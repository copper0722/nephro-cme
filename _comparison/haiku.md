# Regional Anesthesia for AVF Creation: Patency & Cost-Effectiveness

## Summary

Regional anesthesia (brachial plexus block) vs local anesthesia for AVF creation improves primary patency (79% vs 59%, OR 2.7) and functional patency (68% vs 49%) at 12 months. Sympathetic blockade-mediated vasodilation reduces vasospasm and improves early maturation, particularly in radial forearm (RF) fistulas. Cost-effective at £12,900/QALY over 5 years.

---

## Mechanism

**Sympathetic blockade hypothesis**: Regional anesthesia (RA) blocks brachial plexus, abolishing arm vasoconstriction. Early vasodilation ↑ blood flow through nascent AVF, ↓ vasospasm events, → improved maturation kinetics. Localized anesthesia (LA) preserves sympathetic tone, permitting reactive vasospasm during wound handling and early postop period. Functional patency (diameter >6mm, <6mm from skin, flow >600 ml/min) more dependent on unimpeded early flow than primary patency.

Site-specific effect: RF fistulas show robust RA benefit (primary patency 77% vs 48%, functional 78% vs 48%); brachial cephalic (BCF) benefit non-significant. RF-specific mechanism: smaller baseline vessel caliber requires greater proportional flow to achieve functional criteria; RA-induced vasodilation has larger relative impact.

---

## Trial Design & Population

**Aitken et al. (2020), JASN** — observer-blinded RCT, 3 university hospitals Glasgow.
- **N=126** (RA=63, LA=63)
- **Intervention**: Regional = 0.5% L-bupivacaine + 1.5% lidocaine with epinephrine (brachial plexus block) vs Local = 0.5% L-bupivacaine + 1% lidocaine
- **Primary access type**: RF 40%, BCF 60%
- **Primary endpoint**: Primary patency 12mo
- **Secondary endpoints**: Functional patency, cost-effectiveness, maturation kinetics, revisional procedures

## Secondary Outcomes

**12-Month Access Interventions** (cumulative):
- Revisional procedures: 21 total (RA=10, LA=11) — mainly for early thrombosis
- New AVFs created: 53 (RA=23, LA=30) — more common in LA group, suggesting higher primary failure
- Temporary dialysis catheters: 50 (RA=19, LA=31) — more frequent in LA during maturation period

**Late Maturation**: 24 AVFs achieved functional patency between 3–12mo without intervention, suggesting delayed maturation does not always require intervention.

**Adverse Events**:
- No access-related deaths
- Wound infection: 1 (LA)
- Line sepsis: 1 (LA)
- No serious neurologic complications attributed to block

---

## Cost-Effectiveness

**Primary analysis (1 year)**:
- RA net cost saving: £195.10/patient (RA setup/drug cost offset by ↓ revisional procedures & catheters in LA group)
- Functional patency gain: RA +19% absolute → cost *per additional functional AVF* favorable

**5-Year ICER**: £12,900/QALY (below £30,000/QALY threshold)
- **Probability cost-effective at £30,000/QALY**: 89.6%
- Sensitivity analysis robust to parameter variation (drug costs, revision rates, QALY assumptions)

**Budget impact**: Higher anesthetic cost (RA) outweighed by ↓ intervention costs in LA arm. Cumulative cost advantage grows with time horizon.

## Exam Logic

**Why this matters**:
1. **Pathophysiology**: Sympathetic tone ↑ vasospasm in AVF maturation; RA removes this barrier → vasodilation-dependent mechanism (testable in viva)
2. **Site specificity**: RF fistulas most benefit; mechanism = small baseline caliber (RA proportional effect larger). BCF already wider, less vasospasm-dependent
3. **Functional vs primary patency**: Functional requires both patency *and* hemodynamic adequacy (flow, diameter). Early RA-induced dilation improves both; late LA failures = hemodynamic insufficiency, not thrombosis
4. **Cost-effectiveness**: 5yr ICER below threshold; NHS adoption would be economically justified → health policy discussion point
5. **Fragility**: FI=2 is borderline; any loss of 2 participants flips significance. Magnitude of OR (2.7, 2.1) reassuring, but acknowledge small trial size

**Common exam angles**:
- Mechanism of AVF failure (vasospasm vs inadequate flow vs thrombosis)
- Why regional > local for access creation
- Trade-offs: RA complexity vs LA convenience vs long-term functional outcomes
- Cost-effectiveness interpretation (ICER, QALY, threshold)
- Risk assessment in diabetic/older patients (subgroup analysis gap)

## Clinical Implementation

**Patient selection for RA**: 
- RF fistula (strongest benefit)
- Young to middle-aged (cardiopulmonary reserve adequate for block)
- No allergy to amide local anesthetics

**Timing**: Anesthesia must be established before incision and tourniquet application; maintained throughout to preserve vasodilation during critical maturation phase.

**Monitoring**: Functional assessment at 6–8 weeks (Duplex US: diameter, depth, peak systolic velocity, flow); if <600 ml/min or poor maturation trajectory, consider early intervention (aggressive aggressive physical conditioning, angioplasty if stenosis) before abandonment.

---

## Limitations & Generalizability

1. **Single-center (Glasgow)**: All 3 hospitals UK tertiary centers; may not reflect community practice patterns, anesthesia expertise, or patient selection
2. **Operator skill**: Success of RA depends on anesthetist skill; effectiveness variable if technique suboptimal
3. **Age/comorbidity**: Predominantly younger, lower diabetes rate than typical dialysis population; benefit in elderly or high-comorbidity cohorts unclear
4. **Functional patency definition**: >600 ml/min threshold; may not reflect all clinical scenarios (e.g., low-flow fistulas adequate for some patients)
5. **Fragility**: FI=2 limits absolute confidence; larger trial recommended
