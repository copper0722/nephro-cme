# Regional vs Local Anesthesia for AVF Creation: Patency and Cost-Effectiveness

## Overview

**Aitken et al. (2020), JASN** — RCT (N=126, 3 university hospitals, Glasgow). Observer-blinded. Compares **brachial plexus block (RA)** vs **local infiltration (LA)** for surgical AVF creation. Primary AVF mix: RCF 40%, BCF 60%.

---

## Trial Design

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Observer-blinded RCT |
| N | 126 |
| Sites | 3 university hospitals, Glasgow |
| RA protocol | Brachial plexus block: 0.5% L-bupivacaine + 1.5% lidocaine + epinephrine |
| LA protocol | Local infiltration: 0.5% L-bupivacaine + 1% lidocaine |
| AVF distribution | RCF 40%, BCF 60% |
| Follow-up | 12 months |
| Primary endpoint | Functional patency at 12mo |

**Functional patency definition:** vein diameter >6mm, depth <6mm from skin surface, flow >600ml/min — all criteria must be met for HD eligibility.

---

## Primary Results

| Outcome | RA | LA | OR (95% CI) | P | Fragility Index |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary patency 12mo | 79% | 59% | 2.7 (1.6–3.8) | 0.02 | 2 |
| Functional patency 12mo | 68% | 49% | 2.1 (1.5–2.7) | <0.01 | 2 |

**Critical caveat:** Fragility index = 2 → 2 event reversals nullify significance. Results statistically fragile despite P <0.05.

## AVF Type Subgroup

| AVF type | RA primary patency | LA primary patency | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **RCF** | 77% | 48% | Significant |
| **BCF** | NS | NS | Not significant |

RA benefit concentrated in **RCF** (radiocephalic fistula). BCF (brachiocephalic) did not show significant difference — likely due to larger caliber vessels less dependent on sympathetic modulation.

---

## Mechanism

**Sympathetic blockade → vasodilation pathway:**
1. Brachial plexus block → interrupts sympathetic tone to forearm vasculature
2. ↓ vasospasm at anastomosis
3. ↑ early post-operative blood flow
4. ↑ wall shear stress → endothelial remodeling
5. ↑ vein maturation success

**Logistic regression:** anesthetic type = independent predictor of functional patency (OR 0.35, P=0.01), controlling for AVF site. Effect not mediated through AVF type alone.

**Late maturation:** 24 AVFs achieved functional patency between 3–12mo without re-intervention → RA effect may extend vein remodeling window, not just immediate patency.

## Exam Logic

### Why does RA improve AVF patency?
Brachial plexus block → sympathetic denervation → ↓ vasoconstriction → ↑ flow immediately post-anastomosis → exceeded maturation threshold (>600ml/min, >6mm) → successful cannulation. Not analgesia — **vasomotor physiology**.

### Why does RA help RCF more than BCF?
RCF uses smaller caliber vessels (radial artery, cephalic vein at wrist) → more susceptible to vasospasm. BCF uses brachial artery → larger vessel, less sympathetically modulated. RA benefit inversely proportional to vessel caliber.

### What is functional patency vs primary patency?
- **Primary patency:** AVF remains open without intervention
- **Functional patency:** AVF usable for HD (diameter + depth + flow criteria all met)
- Functional patency is the **clinically relevant** endpoint — an open but undersized fistula is useless

### Fragility index = 2: what does this mean?
With FI=2, only 2 patients would need to have had different outcomes to lose statistical significance. Implies the result, though real, is based on a small N and should not be overinterpreted as establishing definitively large effect sizes.

### Cost-effectiveness logic
ICER £12,900/QALY at 5yr is well below NHS £30k WTP threshold → RA is value-for-money even if purchasing specialized anesthesia services. Savings from avoided TDCs, repeat surgeries, and catheter-related complications outweigh upfront RA cost.

### What limits applicability?
1. Single-center (Glasgow) patient demographics — vascular anatomy, ethnicity, DM prevalence may differ
2. Anesthesia expertise required — brachial plexus block is not universal surgical skill
3. FI=2 → replicate before changing standard of care
4. No head-to-head comparison with ultrasound-guided regional techniques

## Clinical Pearls

- **Fistula First, Catheter Last (FFCL)** policy = global standard; RA may be a modifiable surgical factor to improve FFCL compliance rates
- **Late maturation** (3–12mo, n=24) reinforces value of watchful waiting before abandoning AVF → avoid premature TDC insertion
- **Epinephrine** in RA solution: local hemostasis + prolongs block duration; does not negate sympathetic blockade benefit (systemic absorption negligible at doses used)
- **RCF preferred** when anatomically feasible (spares proximal vessels for future access) — RA benefit strongest here, aligning with anatomical preference
- **TDC = surrogate harm endpoint**: each catheter-day = bacteremia risk ≈ 1–3/1000 catheter-days; 50 TDCs in LA group = major morbidity signal
