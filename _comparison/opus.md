# Anesthesia for Arteriovenous Fistula Creation

## Core Concept

**Regional anesthesia (RA)** via brachial plexus block produces **sympathetic blockade** in addition to sensory/motor block. This eliminates perioperative **vasospasm**, increases arterial inflow and venous dilation at the anastomosis, and improves early hemodynamics critical for AVF maturation. **Local anesthesia (LA)** blocks nociception only — sympathetic tone persists, vasospasm risk remains.

**Why this matters for exams:** Vascular access is the Achilles heel of hemodialysis. AVF failure triggers a cascade — tunneled dialysis catheter (TDC) insertion → infection risk → hospitalization → cost. Any modifiable factor improving **functional patency** (not just anatomical patency) is high-yield.

## Definitions

| Term | Definition | Exam Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary patency** | Uninterrupted patency from creation, no intervention | Traditional endpoint; overestimates clinical utility |
| **Functional patency** | Diameter >6 mm, depth <6 mm from skin, flow >600 mL/min; usable for 2-needle cannulation | **Clinically meaningful** — the endpoint that determines whether the patient actually dialyzes through the AVF |
| **Late maturation** | AVF achieving functional criteria between 3-12 months without intervention | Occurs in ~19% of AVFs; argues against premature abandonment |
| **Fragility index** | Minimum number of patients whose event status change would reverse statistical significance | Low FI (e.g., 2) = result is statistically fragile; valid but vulnerable to small perturbations |

## Mechanism: Why RA Works

```
Brachial plexus block
  → sympathetic fiber blockade (C5-T1 dermatomes)
  → arteriolar smooth muscle relaxation
  → ↑ arterial inflow + ↑ venous distension
  → ↓ vasospasm at surgical site
  → ↑ early blood flow through anastomosis
  → ↑ wall shear stress → endothelial remodeling
  → better AVF maturation at 3-12 months
```

**Key pharmacology:** RA uses 0.5% L-bupivacaine + 1.5% lidocaine with epinephrine (long-acting block, 4-8 hours sympatholysis). LA uses subcutaneous infiltration (sensory block only, no sympathetic effect).

## Evidence: Aitken et al. 2020 (JASN)

**Design:** Observer-blinded RCT, 3 university hospitals (Glasgow), N=126. AVF types: **RCF 40%, BCF 60%**.

| Outcome (12 months) | RA | LA | OR (95% CI) | P | Fragility Index |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary patency** | 79% | 59% | 2.7 (1.6-3.8) | 0.02 | 2 |
| **Functional patency** | 68% | 49% | 2.1 (1.5-2.7) | <0.01 | 2 |

**Subgroup by AVF site:**

| AVF Type | RA primary | LA primary | RA functional | LA functional |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **RCF** (radiocephalic) | 77% | 48% | 78% | 48% |
| **BCF** (brachiocephalic) | NS | NS | NS | NS |

**Interpretation:** RA benefit is **concentrated in RCF** (smaller caliber, more vasospasm-prone). BCF already has larger vessels with higher baseline flow — sympatholysis adds less incremental benefit. This is biologically coherent: the smaller the vessel, the greater the relative impact of vasospasm elimination.

**Multivariate logistic regression:** Anesthetic type = independent predictor of patency (OR 0.35, P=0.01) after adjusting for AVF site. This counters the argument that the benefit is merely a surrogate for vessel size.

**Late maturation:** 24 AVFs (19%) achieved functional patency between 3-12 months without surgical revision → supports **waiting before declaring failure**, particularly under RA where maturation trajectory is more favorable.

**Complications at 12 months:**

| Event | RA | LA | Clinical Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revisional procedures | Lower | 21 total | AVF salvage attempts |
| **New AVF creations** | Lower | Higher (53 total) | Failed primary → restart |
| **New TDC insertions** | Lower | Higher (50 total) | Each TDC = infection/thrombosis risk |
| Wound infection | 0 | 1 | — |
| Line sepsis | 0 | 1 | — |
| Access-related death | 0 | 0 | — |

**Cost-effectiveness:**
- RA **net saving £195.10/patient** at 1 year (lower reintervention + fewer TDCs offsets higher anesthetic cost)
- 5-year ICER: **£12,900/QALY** (well below £30,000 threshold)
- Probability cost-effective at £30,000/QALY: **89.6%**
- Dominant strategy at 1 year (cheaper AND more effective)

## Exam Logic

### High-Yield Question Stems

1. **"Which anesthetic technique improves AVF functional patency?"** → RA (brachial plexus block). Mechanism = sympathetic blockade → vasodilation.

2. **"RA benefit is most pronounced in which AVF configuration?"** → **Radiocephalic fistula** (smaller vessel, more vasospasm-susceptible). BCF shows no significant benefit.

3. **"What is functional patency?"** → Composite of diameter >6 mm, depth <6 mm, flow >600 mL/min. Distinguished from primary patency (anatomical openness without intervention).

4. **"A patient's AVF is not mature at 6 weeks. Next step?"** → **Wait** (not immediate revision). Late maturation (3-12 months) occurs in ~19% without intervention. Serial duplex US monitoring.

5. **"What is the fragility index and why does it matter?"** → Minimum event changes to reverse significance. FI=2 means if 2 patients in the RA group had failed instead of succeeded, the P-value would cross 0.05. The result is **statistically significant but fragile** — valid for hypothesis generation, requires confirmation in larger trials.

### Common Distractors

| Distractor | Why Wrong |
|---|---|
| "RA only improves intraoperative flow, no long-term benefit" | Refuted: 12-month primary AND functional patency significantly higher |
| "RA is more expensive" | Wrong at system level: fewer TDCs + fewer reinterventions = net saving at 1 year |
| "General anesthesia is needed for AVF creation" | AVF is a superficial procedure; GA unnecessary. Choice is RA vs LA |
| "BCF benefits equally from RA" | No: BCF subgroup showed no significant difference. RA benefit = RCF-driven |
| "Functional patency = primary patency" | Functional patency requires usability criteria (diameter, depth, flow). Primary patency = merely open |

### Causal Reasoning Check

- **Counterfactual:** Would these AVFs have matured equally under LA? The RCT design (randomization) addresses confounding, but FI=2 means the counterfactual conclusion is fragile.
- **Competing risk:** Death, transplantation, or PD switch could censor AVF outcomes. No access-related deaths reported; competing risks appear balanced.
- **Collider:** Analyzing only "mature" AVFs would introduce collider bias. The trial correctly uses intention-to-treat.
- **Time-zero:** Randomization at surgery = clean time-zero. No immortal time bias.

## Clinical Bottom Line

For **radiocephalic AVF creation**, brachial plexus block (RA) should be preferred over local anesthesia. The **NNT ≈ 5** for functional patency at 12 months (68% vs 49% → absolute risk reduction ~19%), with concurrent cost savings. For **brachiocephalic AVF**, RA shows no significant benefit — LA is sufficient. The fragility index of 2 warrants confirmatory trials before guideline-level recommendation, but the biological plausibility (sympatholysis → vasodilation → maturation) is strong.
