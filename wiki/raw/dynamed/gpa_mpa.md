# GPA and MPA (DynaMed)

Updated 06 Apr 2026. 97 pages.

## Overview
GPA and MPA are the most common ANCA-associated vasculitides (AAV). Small-vessel necrotizing vasculitis with few/no immune deposits. Prevalence 200-400/million (AAV overall); GPA 2.3-140/million; MPA 2.7-94/million. Peak onset 65-74; M=F; more common in White/Northern European populations. GPA = necrotizing granulomatous vasculitis (upper+lower airway, kidneys). MPA = necrotizing vasculitis without granulomas (kidneys, lungs).

## GPA vs MPA
GPA: c-ANCA/PR3 in 80-90%; upper airway involvement 70-100% (sinusitis, saddle nose, subglottic stenosis); lung nodules/cavities/hemorrhage; kidney ~70%; granulomas on biopsy; ocular 50%; CV up to 44%. MPA: p-ANCA/MPO in ~60%; NO upper airway; kidney >90% (more severe); DAH/ILD; no granulomas; more severe renal disease. Both: prodromes (myalgias, arthralgias, fatigue, weight loss, fever) for weeks-months before organ-specific manifestations.

## ANCA Testing
c-ANCA (PR3): diffuse cytoplasmic granular fluorescence; 80-90% GPA, ~25% MPA. p-ANCA (MPO): perinuclear staining; 5-20% GPA, ~60% MPA. a-ANCA: atypical; drug-induced, IBD, RA. Use antigen-specific immunoassay (PR3/MPO ELISA) as PRIMARY screening [Strong Recommendation per 2017 International Consensus]. ANCA positive in >90% GPA and up to 90% MPA. Negative ANCA does not exclude (up to 20% GPA, 10% MPA may be negative).

## Classification Criteria (ACR/EULAR 2022)
GPA (>=5 points): nasal symptoms +3, cartilaginous involvement +2, hearing loss +1, c-ANCA/PR3 +5, pulmonary nodules/cavitation +2, granuloma/giant cells on biopsy +2, paranasal sinus inflammation +1, pauci-immune GN +1; p-ANCA/MPO -1; eosinophils >=1x10^9/L -4. MPA (>=5 points): nasal involvement -3, p-ANCA/MPO +6, fibrosis/ILD on chest imaging +3, pauci-immune GN +3, c-ANCA/PR3 -1, eosinophils >=1x10^9/L -4.

## Risk Factors
Genetic: HLA-DPB1*04 (GPA risk), HLA-DRB1*09:01 (MPA in Japanese); PRTN3, SERPINA1, SEMA6A, CTLA-4 genes. Genomic signature correlates more with ANCA specificity than clinical phenotype. Environmental: silica (OR 2.57 for AAV), smoking (associated with MPO-ANCA vasculitis; OR 1.7 former, 2.7 current), solvents, heavy metals. Infections: S. aureus (GPA). Drugs: anti-thyroid (benzylthiouracil, carbimazole, methimazole, propylthiouracil), hydralazine, anti-TNF, cocaine/levamisole, allopurinol.

## Organ Involvement
Kidney: ~70% GPA, >90% MPA; often asymptomatic (urinalysis screening); HTN, hematuria, proteinuria, rising Cr; pauci-immune crescentic GN. Lung: GPA (nodules, cavities, hemorrhage, subglottic/tracheal stenosis); MPA (DAH, ILD, capillaritis). ENT (GPA only): recurrent sinusitis, nasal crusting/bloody discharge, septal perforation, saddle nose, otitis media, hearing loss, subglottic stenosis. Eyes: scleritis, conjunctivitis, uveitis, proptosis (orbital granuloma in GPA). Skin: purpura, skin ulcers, livedo reticularis, nodules (35% GPA, 30-60% MPA). Neuro: mononeuritis multiplex, peripheral neuropathy, CNS involvement (GPA>MPA). GI: 6.5% (abdominal pain, GI bleeding). CV: up to 44% GPA.

## Diagnosis
Comprehensive: history + ANCA (PR3 and MPO) + biopsy of affected organ + imaging. Biopsy: renal shows necrotizing crescentic GN; nasal/lung shows necrotizing granulomatous vasculitis (GPA). CXR: infiltrates, nodules, ground-glass. Blood: elevated CRP/ESR, normochromic normocytic anemia, thrombocytosis, elevated Cr. UA: hematuria, proteinuria, RBC casts.

## Treatment (KDIGO 2021 + EULAR/ERA-EDTA 2024)
Induction (organ-threatening): glucocorticoids + rituximab OR CYC [Strong]. Rituximab preferred (RAVE, RITUXVAS trials). CYC preferred if severe renal (Cr >300-500 mcmol/L). Methylprednisolone 500-1000 mg IV x3 days then oral taper. Rituximab: 375 mg/m2 weekly x4 or 1g x2. CYC: 2 mg/kg/day oral x 3-6 months (reduce in elderly/renal impairment). Adjuncts: PLEX if Cr >300-500 [Conditional]; avacopan (C5a receptor inhibitor) to reduce steroid exposure [Conditional]; IVIG for refractory GPA [Conditional]. Maintenance: rituximab 500 mg q6 months (MAINRITSAN) preferred [Conditional]; alternatives: azathioprine, methotrexate, MMF. CYC NOT for maintenance. Duration: 6-12 months after complete remission. TMP-SMX prophylaxis [Strong]. Monitor ANCA monthly then q1-3 months.
