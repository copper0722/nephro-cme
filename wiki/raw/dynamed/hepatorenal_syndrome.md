# Hepatorenal Syndrome (DynaMed)

## Definition
- Functional renal impairment in patients with advanced liver disease/cirrhosis + portal hypertension
- Diagnosis of exclusion: bland urine sediment, urine Na < 20 mEq/L, progressive GFR decline
- HRS-AKI (type 1): rapid decline, Cr doubling to > 2.5 mg/dL in < 2 weeks; high mortality
- Type 2 HRS (non-AKI HRS / HRS-CKD): slow progressive decline; Cr typically > 1.5 mg/dL; refractory ascites

## ICA Diagnostic Criteria (Current)
- Cirrhosis with ascites
- AKI present (SCr increase >= 0.3 mg/dL in <= 48h OR >= 50% increase over 7 days)
- Failed response to 48h albumin volume expansion (1 g/kg) + diuretic discontinuation
- Absence of shock
- No current/recent nephrotoxic drugs
- No structural kidney injury (proteinuria < 500 mg/d, no microhematuria, normal renal US)
- Key practice point: cellular or muddy brown casts on UA → exclude HRS

## ICA AKI Staging in Cirrhosis
- Stage 1: SCr increase >= 0.3 mg/dL over <= 48h OR >= 1.5-2x baseline
  - Stage 1A: SCr < 1.5 mg/dL → vasoconstrictors not yet indicated
  - Stage 1B: SCr >= 1.5 mg/dL → start vasoconstrictors + albumin
- Stage 2: SCr > 2-3x baseline
- Stage 3: SCr > 3x baseline OR >= 4 mg/dL OR initiation of RRT

## Epidemiology
- ~11% of hospitalized patients with decompensated cirrhosis + refractory ascites
- 10-30% of cirrhosis patients with SBP develop HRS
- Non-HRS AKI: 25-50% of acute decompensated cirrhosis
- Prerenal (including HRS): 60-70% of all AKI in cirrhosis; HRS-AKI specifically 11-20%
- Intrinsic causes: ~30% (ATN, AIN, GN, bile acid nephropathy)
- Postrenal: < 1%

## Pathogenesis
- Cirrhosis + portal hypertension → splanchnic vasodilation → reduced effective arterial volume → RAAS/SNS/AVP activation → renal vasoconstriction → decreased GFR
- Bacterial translocation → inflammatory cytokines → further renal impairment
- Cirrhotic cardiomyopathy (up to 50%): inadequate cardiac compensation
- Adrenal insufficiency (25% of decompensated cirrhosis)
- Bile acid nephropathy: tubular damage from bile acid accumulation

## Clinical Presentation
- Type 1 (HRS-AKI): rapid deterioration, often with precipitant (SBP, GI bleed, large volume paracentesis)
- Type 2 (non-AKI HRS): slow, refractory ascites, moderate renal impairment
- Signs of liver disease: ascites, jaundice, edema, hypoalbuminemia, coagulopathy, hepatic encephalopathy
- Hypervolemic hyponatremia common

## Evaluation
- Blood: SCr, BUN, Na (hyponatremia in HRS), osmolality, cystatin C, CBC, LFTs, blood cultures
- Urine: Na < 10 mEq/L (< 1% FENa; some suggest < 0.1% in cirrhosis), osmolality > plasma, sediment exam, UPCR
- NGAL: may help differentiate ATN vs HRS (AUC 0.87 at cutoff 220 mcg/g Cr on day 3)
- KIM-1: elevated in HRS risk (PPV 75%, NPV 84% at cutoff 1.499 ng/mL)
- FEurea: decreased = hypoperfusion (HRS); increased = tubular injury (ATN); less affected by diuretics than FENa
- Imaging: Doppler US (renal arterial resistance index > 0.7 → increased HRS risk); rule out obstruction
- Paracentesis: rule out SBP (ascitic fluid neutrophils, protein, albumin for SAAG)

## Management
- STOP all nephrotoxins (NSAIDs, ACEi, ARBs, diuretics, aminoglycosides)
- Dose all medications assuming GFR < 15 mL/min/1.73 m2

### Type 1 HRS (HRS-AKI)
- Vasoconstrictors + albumin for HRS-AKI > stage 1A (SCr >= 1.5 mg/dL) [Strong]
- **Terlipressin + albumin = first-line** [Strong/EASL Grade 1, Level I]
  - V1 receptor agonist → splanchnic vasoconstriction → improved renal perfusion
  - CONFIRM trial (NEJM 2021): terlipressin vs placebo; HRS reversal 32% vs 17% (NNT 7); but respiratory failure 11% vs 2%
- Norepinephrine + albumin: alternative to terlipressin [Conditional/EASL Grade 2, Level I]
- Midodrine + octreotide + albumin: only if terlipressin AND norepinephrine unavailable [Strong/EASL Grade 1, Level I]; less effective

### Type 2 HRS (non-AKI HRS)
- Vasoconstrictors + albumin NOT recommended [Strong/EASL Grade 1, Level I]
- May respond to terlipressin but recurrence common after stopping

### Definitive Treatment
- Liver transplant: best therapy regardless of drug response [Strong/EASL Grade 1, Level I]
- Simultaneous liver-kidney transplant: for CKD, sustained AKI, or metabolic disease meeting criteria
- RRT: based on severity [Conditional/EASL Grade 2, Level I]
- TIPS: may be considered for type 2 HRS [Conditional]

## Prevention
- Albumin for SBP: reduces HRS incidence
- Avoid large volume paracentesis without albumin replacement (6-8 g per liter removed)
