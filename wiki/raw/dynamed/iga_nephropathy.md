# IgA Nephropathy (DynaMed)

Updated 19 Jan 2026. 84 pages.

## Overview
Most common primary GN worldwide. Characterized by varying degrees of glomerular hematuria, proteinuria, and progressive CKD. Kidney biopsy showing IgA-dominant mesangial deposits required for diagnosis. Incidence 0.06-10.5/100K (highest in SE Asia, Australia, Japan). M:F 2-6:1. Peak 2nd-3rd decade. 25-30% progress to ESKD over 20-25 years.

## Pathogenesis
Multi-hit process: (1) increased galactose-deficient IgA1 (Gd-IgA1) from mucosal plasma cells, (2) anti-Gd-IgA1 autoantibodies form immune complexes, (3) Gd-IgA1-auto-Ab immune complexes deposit in mesangium, (4) mesangial cell activation + complement activation -> inflammation, proliferation, fibrosis. Genetic predisposition (~5% have affected family member). 30 genome-wide risk loci identified. IgAN secondary to cirrhosis from impaired hepatic IgA clearance.

## Types
Primary: no underlying cause. Secondary: liver disease (cirrhosis 9-25% of biopsies at liver transplant, HBV 30% biopsy-confirmed), HCV, HIV, celiac disease, IBD, seronegative arthritis, lymphoma, infections.

## Clinical Presentations
Asymptomatic microscopic hematuria (most common; proteinuria <0.5 g/day). Synpharyngitic macroscopic hematuria (10-15%; gross hematuria CONCURRENT with pharyngitis; age <40). IgAN with MCD (nephrotic syndrome with preserved GFR). IgAN with AKI (gross hematuria + upper respiratory infection). Crescentic IgAN/RPGN (>=50% GFR decline in <=3 months). Chronic progressive (HTN, rising Cr, worsening proteinuria). Up to 40% present with ESKD.

## Diagnosis
Suspect: persistent microscopic hematuria + proteinuria >=0.5 g/day + macroscopic hematuria with URI + HTN + declining GFR. Confirm: kidney biopsy showing IgA-dominant mesangial deposits. No validated serum/urine biomarker. Elevated serum IgA (>=315 mg/dL in ~50%) and IgA/C3 ratio are NOT diagnostic. Biopsy indicated if proteinuria >=0.5 g/day. Biopsy may not be needed if asymptomatic hematuria + minimal proteinuria <0.5 g/day.

## Biopsy Findings
LM: mesangial hypercellularity (>=4 cells/area), segmental endocapillary proliferation, segmental sclerosis, crescents. IF: IgA dominant mesangial; weak IgG/IgM may coexist. EM: mesangial/paramesangial electron-dense deposits (occasionally subepithelial/subendothelial). Oxford MEST-C classification: M (mesangial hypercellularity 0/1), E (endocapillary proliferation 0/1), S (segmental sclerosis 0/1), T (tubular atrophy/interstitial fibrosis 0/1/2), C (crescents 0/1/2).

## Treatment (KDIGO 2021)
All: ACEi/ARB max tolerated dose [Strong]; sparsentan [Conditional]; SGLT2i [Conditional]; BP <=120/70; dietary Na <2 g/day. If at risk of progression (proteinuria >=0.5 g/day): consider glucocorticoids 6-9 months [Conditional] or targeted-release budesonide (Nefecon) 9-month course [Conditional]. eGFR <=30: glucocorticoids with caution. Crescentic IgAN: CYC + steroids (similar to ANCA vasculitis). Not recommended: rituximab, azathioprine, CNI (insufficient evidence). Secondary IgAN: treat underlying cause. Children: similar approach with immunosuppression threshold lower.
