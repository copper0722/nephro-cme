# Immunosuppression in Renal Transplantation (DynaMed)

## Induction Immunosuppression
- Goal: reduce incidence/severity of acute rejection in early post-transplant period
- Start combination before or at time of transplant [Strong]
- Include biologic agent [Strong]
- IL-2R antagonists (basiliximab) = first-line induction [Strong/KDIGO 1B]
- Lymphocyte-depleting agents (rabbit ATG, alemtuzumab) for high immunologic risk [Conditional/KDIGO 2B]

### Risk Assessment for Induction Selection
| Factor | Low Risk | High Risk |
|---|---|---|
| HLA mismatch | Zero | Increased |
| Donor | Live | Deceased, older donor age |
| Ethnicity | Caucasian | African American |
| PRA | Low | High |
| DSA | Absent | Present |
| ABO compatibility | Yes | No |
| Graft function | Immediate | Delayed |
| Cold ischemia | Short | Long |
| Transplant number | First | Retransplant |

### Lymphocyte-Depleting Agents
#### Rabbit ATG (Thymoglobulin)
- Polyclonal anti-T cell antibodies; depletes T lymphocytes
- Dose: 1.5 mg/kg IV daily x 7-14 days; first dose intraoperatively
- Benefits: rapid withdrawal of steroids, late introduction of CNI, lower acute rejection
- ATG vs IL-2Ra: BPAR 11.8% vs 20.9% at 12 months (p = 0.0057, NNT 11)
- AEs: leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, infections, fever, cytokine release syndrome, serum sickness (0.25%), PTLD (rare)
- Dose adjust: reduce 50% if WBC 2,000-3,000 or platelet 50,000-75,000; hold if WBC < 2,000 or platelet < 50,000

#### Alemtuzumab (Campath)
- Anti-CD52 humanized mAb; 30 mg IV single dose
- NOT FDA-approved for transplant (off-label via Campath Distribution Program)
- Intense/prolonged lymphocyte depletion
- Reduced BPAR vs basiliximab in low-risk (2% vs 18% at 6 months, NNT 7) but not high-risk patients
- AEs: serious infections (35% vs 22% vs basiliximab in low-risk), late ABMR
- Higher risk of death vs ATG: HR 1.14 (JASN 2017)

### Basiliximab (Simulect)
- Chimeric anti-IL-2R-alpha mAb; inhibits IL-2-mediated T cell proliferation
- Dose: 20 mg IV x 2 doses (within 2h pre-transplant + day 4)
- Used in low immunologic risk patients
- IL-2R antagonist vs placebo/no treatment: reduced graft loss (RR 0.75, NNT 24-91), BPAR (RR 0.72, NNT 8-17), CMV (RR 0.81, NNT 21-223)
- IL-2Ra vs ATG: higher BPAR (RR 1.3, NNT 10-666) but less malignancy (RR 0.25)

## Maintenance Immunosuppression
- Triple therapy: CNI + antiproliferative + corticosteroid [KDIGO 1B]
- Tacrolimus = first-line CNI [KDIGO 2A]
- MMF = first-line antiproliferative [KDIGO 2B]
- Start CNI before or at transplant (not delayed) [KDIGO 2D/2B]
- Low risk + induction: consider steroid D/C in first week [KDIGO 2B]
- mTOR inhibitors: only after graft function established + wounds healed [Strong]
- Lowest planned immunosuppression typically reached 6-12 months

### Calcineurin Inhibitors (CNIs)
- Cyclosporine: microemulsion preferred; C2 monitoring; dose 6-18 mg/kg/day
- Tacrolimus: 0.2 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses; C0 (trough) monitoring
- Target levels vary by time post-transplant and induction type (see tables)
- AEs: nephrotoxicity (afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction, isometric vacuolization on biopsy), hypertension, hyperkalemia, hypomagnesemia, hypophosphatemia, neurotoxicity, hyperglycemia/PTDM (more with tacrolimus), gingival hyperplasia (cyclosporine only), hyperlipidemia
- Narrow therapeutic window → TDM mandatory
- Drug interactions: CYP3A4/P-gp substrates; macrolides, azole antifungals, grapefruit, nondihydropyridine CCBs, protease inhibitors → INCREASE CNI levels; phenytoin, carbamazepine, phenobarbital, rifampin → DECREASE

### Tacrolimus Target Trough Levels
| Time | No Induction | IL-2R Induction | ATG Induction | mTORi Maintenance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0-3 months | 10-15 ng/mL | 10-15 (first 6 wk) then 5-10 | 5-10 | 3-7 |
| 3-12 months | 5-15 | 5-10 (may use 3-7) | 5-10 | 3-7 |
| > 12 months | 5-10 | 5-10 (may use 3-7) | 5-10 | 3-7 |

### Belatacept (Nulojix)
- Selective costimulation blocker (CD80/86 on APC → blocks CD28 on T cell)
- Only for EBV-seropositive patients (PTLD risk if EBV-negative)
- CNI-free regimen → improved renal function vs cyclosporine
- Higher rate of acute rejection but better long-term GFR

### mTOR Inhibitors (Sirolimus, Everolimus)
- Inhibit mTOR → block IL-2-mediated T cell proliferation
- AEs: hyperlipidemia, proteinuria, impaired wound healing, oral ulcers, pneumonitis, cytopenias
- Use after wounds healed [Strong]; may convert from CNI for nephrotoxicity
- Consider if eGFR > 40 + proteinuria < 500 mg/g [Conditional]

### Antiproliferative Agents
- MMF (mycophenolate mofetil) / MPA (mycophenolic acid): inhibit IMPDH → block purine synthesis in lymphocytes
- Azathioprine: 6-MP prodrug; less effective than MMF; consider switching to MMF after rejection
- AEs: GI (diarrhea, nausea), leukopenia, anemia
- Allopurinol + azathioprine: contraindicated (severe myelosuppression)

## Treatment of Rejection
### Acute TCMR
- Corticosteroids = first-line [Strong]; methylprednisolone pulse 250-500 mg IV x 3 days
- Add/restore prednisone maintenance after treatment [Conditional]
- Grade 2A+ or steroid-resistant: lymphocyte-depleting antibodies [Conditional]
- Switch azathioprine to MMF [Conditional]

### Acute ABMR
- Treatment with >= 1 of (with or without steroids) [Conditional]:
  - Plasma exchange or plasmapheresis
  - IVIG
  - Anti-CD20 antibody (rituximab)
  - Lymphocyte-depleting antibody

### Subclinical Rejection
- Treatment suggested [Conditional]; corticosteroids may be used

### Chronic Allograft Injury
- Biopsy all patients with declining function [Strong]
- Consider reducing/replacing CNI if histological CNI toxicity [Conditional]
- Consider CNI → mTOR if eGFR > 40 + proteinuria < 500 mg/g [Conditional]
