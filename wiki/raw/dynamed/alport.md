# Alport Syndrome (DynaMed)

Updated 27 May 2025. 27 pages.

## Overview
Genetic disorder of type IV collagen (alpha-3/4/5 chains) in GBM, cochlea, lens. No curative treatment. Characterized by progressive renal failure + sensorineural hearing loss + ocular abnormalities. Prevalence 1:5,000 (gene mutation); 1:10,000 (XLAS); 1:50,000 (ARAS).

## Genetics
XLAS (80%): COL4A5 on Xq22.3; males severely affected, females variable. ARAS (15%): COL4A3/COL4A4 on 2q36.3, biallelic; both sexes equally severe, ESRD <30. ADAS (5%): COL4A3/COL4A4 single heterozygous; mildest, slower progression. Single COL4A3/COL4A4 mutations can cause either ADAS or thin basement membrane nephropathy (TBMN = carrier state for ARAS; benign).

## Staging
Stage 0: microscopic hematuria, no microalbuminuria. Stage 1: microalbuminuria (30-300 mg/g). Stage 2: gross proteinuria (>300 mg/g). Stage 3: GFR <60. Stage 4: ESRD.

## Clinical Features
Hematuria: 100% males (XLAS/ARAS), >90% XLAS carrier females (intermittent). Proteinuria: develops later in childhood/adolescence; all XLAS males develop. Hearing loss: sensorineural, bilateral, medium-high frequency (2000-8000 Hz); not congenital; XLAS 80-90% males by age 40, 45% females; ARAS 66%. Ocular: anterior lenticonus (pathognomonic, 10-40% XLAS/ARAS, NOT in XLAS females), perimacular flecks (30-50%, very high specificity), posterior cataract, corneal dystrophy.

## Mutation Type Correlation (XLAS)
Large deletion: ESRD 66.7%, median age 22; hearing loss 80.8%; ocular 50%. Missense: ESRD 54.8%, median age 37; hearing loss 50.8%; ocular 20.3%. Small deletion: ESRD 75%, age 22. Truncating: ESRD 59.7%, age 25; hearing loss 95.5%; ocular 50%.

## Diagnosis
Genetic testing = gold standard (COL4A3/A4/A5). Kidney biopsy EM: lamellation ("basket weave") of GBM lamina densa; variable thickness. Immunohistochemistry: absent alpha-3/4/5(IV) staining in ~80% XLAS males; patchy in XLAS females; absent alpha-3/4 in GBM but positive in Bowman capsule/TBM in ARAS. Skin biopsy: absent alpha-5(IV) on epidermal BM (86% sensitivity XLAS males). Audiometry: bilateral 2000-8000 Hz reduction.

## Differential
TBMN: uniformly thin GBM (<250 nm), no lamellation, no extrarenal findings; same genes (carrier state). IgAN: mesangial IgA. Fechtner: nephritis + deafness + thrombocytopenia + leukocyte inclusions (MYH9). Nail-patella: FSGS + nail/patella dysplasia (LMX1B).

## Treatment
RAAS blockade: ACEi first-line (ramipril 1-2 mg/m2/day); start at overt proteinuria or microalbuminuria in high-risk boys. Target UPCR <0.5 + >=50% reduction. ARB second-line (add to ACEi or substitute). Spironolactone third-line. RAAS inhibitors decrease ESRD risk (HR 0.33). ACEi delayed dialysis from age 22 to 40 (Kidney Int 2012). SGLT2i: emerging data, albuminuria decreased (Kidney Int Rep 2024). Transplant: similar prognosis to other ESRD; risk of de novo anti-GBM (3-4%, more with large deletions). Donor selection: optimal = unaffected male; female carriers >=40 with normal function may donate.
