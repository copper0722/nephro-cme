# Dialysis for Acute Poisonings (DynaMed)

## General Principles
- ECTR indicated for ~0.1% of poisonings (severe toxicity)
- IHD = preferred modality [Strong]; CRRT if hemodynamically unstable
- IHD: removes small molecules (< 5,000 Da), non-protein-bound, low Vd; clearance 200-250 mL/min
- CRRT: slower clearance (2-3x less than IHD per hour), but suitable for unstable patients
- Hemoperfusion: charcoal/resin column; removes protein-bound + larger molecules; higher complication rate

## Toxin Characteristics Favoring Dialysis
- Low molecular weight (< 500-1,000 Da for HD; up to 50,000 Da for HF)
- Low protein binding (free drug dialyzed)
- Low volume of distribution (< 1-2 L/kg; drug in vascular space)
- High water solubility
- Low endogenous clearance (< 4 mL/min/kg)

## EXTRIP Dialyzability Categories
| Category | Toxin Removed in 6h ECTR |
|---|---|
| Dialyzable | > 30% |
| Moderately dialyzable | 10-30% |
| Slightly dialyzable | 3-10% |
| Not dialyzable | < 3% |

## Impaired Renal Function Definition (EXTRIP)
- CKD stage 3B+ (eGFR < 45 mL/min/1.73 m2)
- KDIGO AKI stage 2-3
- SCr >= 2 mg/dL (adults), >= 1.5 mg/dL (elderly/low muscle mass)
- > 2x ULN for age/sex
- Oliguria/anuria > 6 hours

## Specific Poisonings — ECTR Indications

### Ethylene Glycol (Dialyzable)
- Half-life 2-5h without antidote; ECTR clearance ~200 mL/min
- Provide ECTR if [Strong]: concentration > 50 mmol/L + ethanol; > 10 mmol/L + no antidote; glycolate > 12 mmol/L; AG > 27 mmol/L; coma/seizures/AKI stage 2-3
- Stop: AG < 18 mmol/L [Strong]; or EG < 4 mmol/L + acid-base balance achieved
- Treatment: fomepizole (preferred) or ethanol + ECTR

### Methanol (Dialyzable)
- Half-life 14-30h (43-96h with ethanol); ECTR clearance ~208 mL/min
- Provide ECTR if [Strong]: coma, seizures, new visual deficits; metabolic acidosis pH <= 7.15 despite treatment; methanol > 700 mg/L (fomepizole), > 600 mg/L (ethanol), < 500 mg/L (no ADH inhibitor); impaired renal function; AG > 24 mmol/L
- Stop: methanol < 200 mg/L + clinical improvement [Strong]

### Lithium (Dialyzable, EXTRIP Grade A)
- Endogenous clearance 15-20 mL/min; HD clearance 70-170 mL/min
- Provide ECTR if [Strong]: severe toxicity; impaired renal function + Li > 4 mEq/L; decreased consciousness/seizures/dysrhythmias (regardless of level)
- Consider ECTR if [Strong]: Li > 5 mEq/L; confusion; expected > 36h to reach < 1 mEq/L
- Stop: clinical improvement OR Li < 1 mEq/L [Strong]; if unable to measure, stop after >= 6h [Strong]
- Rebound common → may need repeat sessions

### Salicylate (Dialyzable, EXTRIP Grade B)
- Half-life 2-4h at therapeutic levels; ECTR clearance > 100 mL/min
- Provide ECTR if [Strong]: level > 7.2 mmol/L (100 mg/dL); > 6.5 mmol/L (90 mg/dL) if impaired renal function; altered mental status; new hypoxemia requiring supplemental O2
- Consider ECTR if standard therapy fails + level > 6.5 mmol/L (90 mg/dL) if impaired renal function, or pH <= 7.2
- Stop: level < 1.4 mmol/L (19 mg/dL) [Strong]; or after 4-6h if level unavailable [Conditional]
- First-line: urinary alkalinization; add ECTR for severe cases

### Metformin (Moderately Dialyzable, EXTRIP Grade C)
- Endogenous clearance 400-650 mL/min; ECTR clearance > 200 mL/min
- Provide ECTR if [Strong]: lactate > 20 mmol/L (180 mg/dL); pH <= 7; refractory to standard therapy
- Consider ECTR if: lactate 15-20 mmol/L (135-180 mg/dL) or pH 7-7.1 [Conditional]
- Continue until: lactate < 27 mg/dL (3 mmol/L) + pH > 7.35 [Strong]
- Use bicarbonate buffer for HD

### Acetaminophen (Dialyzable, EXTRIP Grade C)
- Half-life 1-2h; ECTR clearance 73-215 mL/min
- Consider ECTR if [Conditional]: severe poisoning; if NAC not given: level > 1,000 mg/L [Strong] or > 800 mg/L [Conditional]; altered mental status + metabolic acidosis + increased lactate + level > 700 mg/L [Strong] or > 500 mg/L [Conditional]
- NAC administered + level > 900 mg/L [Strong] or > 800 mg/L [Conditional]
- Stop: clinical improvement [Strong]

### Valproic Acid (Moderately Dialyzable)
- Provide ECTR if [Strong]: severe poisoning; level > 1,300 mg/L (9,000 mcmol/L) + shock/cerebral edema
- Consider if: level > 900 mg/L (6,250 mcmol/L); coma/respiratory depression; acute hyperammonemia; pH <= 7.1
- Stop: clinical improvement OR level 50-100 mg/L [Conditional]

### Carbamazepine
- Provide ECTR if: refractory seizures or life-threatening dysrhythmias [Strong]
- Consider if: prolonged coma or respiratory depression [Conditional]
- Stop: clinical improvement OR level < 10 mg/L [Strong/Conditional]

### Phenytoin
- Consider ECTR if: severe poisoning with prolonged coma or ataxia [Conditional]
- Stop: clinical improvement [Strong]

### Barbiturates
- Provide ECTR for severe long-acting barbiturate poisoning [Strong]: prolonged coma [Strong], refractory shock [Strong], post MDAC and toxicity persists [Strong/Conditional], mechanical ventilation required [Conditional]
- Stop: clinical improvement [Strong]

## Other Dialyzable Poisons
- Theophylline, diethylene glycol, propylene glycol, isopropyl alcohol
- Paraquat, star fruit, hair dye (paraphenylenediamine)

## NOT Dialyzable (ECTR Not Indicated)
- Hydrocarbon, anticholinergic, arsenic (chronic), carbon monoxide, cyanide, lead, opioids, tricyclic antidepressants

## Rebound Phenomenon
- After ECTR cessation, plasma toxin level may rise as drug redistributes from tissue
- Management: extend dialysis > 4h; repeat sessions; consider continuous therapy for high-Vd substances
