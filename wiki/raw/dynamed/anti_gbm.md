# Anti-GBM Disease (DynaMed)

Updated 10 Oct 2024. 24 pages.

## Overview
Rare autoimmune disease: anti-GBM antibodies cause RPGN and/or pulmonary hemorrhage. Incidence 0.5-1/million. Antibodies target alpha-3 chain of type IV collagen NC1 domain. Bimodal peaks: 20-30 (males, pulmonary+renal) and 60-70 (females, renal-limited). 60-80% both kidney+lung; 20-40% kidney only; <10% lung only. Up to 20% of RPGN. ANCA co-positivity in 27% (usually MPO).

## Pathogenesis
Injury to pulmonary vasculature or GBM exposes alpha-3 NC1 epitopes -> circulating anti-GBM antibodies -> bind GBM/alveolar BM -> complement activation -> crescentic GN + alveolar hemorrhage. "Conformeropathy": quaternary structure of alpha345NC1 hexamer controls epitope exposure. May involve autoantigen complementarity.

## Risk Factors
Pulmonary injury: smoking, cocaine, hydrocarbon solvents, hard metals (tungsten, carbide, cobalt), respiratory infections (influenza), alemtuzumab. Genetic: HLA-DR15, HLA-DRB1*03, HLA-DRB1*04. Alport syndrome post-transplant (3-4%; large gene deletions; exposed to "normal" alpha-3/4/5 collagen IV).

## Clinical Presentation
Dyspnea 79%, hemoptysis 75%, fatigue 64%, cough 64%, hematuria 36%, edema 32%, fever 43%, weight loss 18%, arthralgia 18%. Smokers: pulmonary+renal. Non-smokers: renal-limited. Rapid onset 1 week to 1 month before hospitalization. Dual anti-GBM+ANCA (27%): mortality 35.2% at 1 year, 44.4% at 5 years.

## Diagnosis
Serology: anti-GBM antibodies by ELISA/Western blot (sensitivity 93%, specificity 97%). Chemiluminescence immunoassay: sensitivity 95.6%, specificity 99.6%. Biopsy: linear IgG along GBM + crescentic GN. Always check ANCA. CXR: bilateral perihilar consolidation (may be normal). HRCT: diffuse alveolar hemorrhage. Bronchoscopy: increasingly bloody returns.

## Treatment (KDIGO 2021 - EMERGENCY)
Triple therapy: (1) methylprednisolone 500-1000 mg/day IV x3 days -> prednisone 1 mg/kg/day, taper to 20 mg by 6 weeks, stop by 6 months; (2) cyclophosphamide 2-3 mg/kg/day oral x 2-3 months; (3) plasmapheresis: daily 50 mL/kg (max 4L) with albumin x 14 days or until anti-GBM undetectable. Start immediately if suspected (before confirmation). If pulmonary hemorrhage: treat regardless of kidney prognosis [Strong]. Dialysis-dependent + >85% crescents + no pulmonary hemorrhage: dialysis alone (kidney recovery unlikely). No maintenance needed (relapse <=6%). Defer transplant until anti-GBM undetectable >=6 months. Rituximab or MMF for refractory (limited evidence). Immunoadsorption may be alternative to PLEX.

## Prognosis
Untreated: almost always fatal. With treatment: >80% 5-year survival, <30% need long-term dialysis. Poor prognostic factors: Cr >4 mg/dL, oligoanuria, >50% crescents, dialysis-dependent at presentation. 1-year kidney survival: 95% if Cr <500 mcmol/L; 82% if Cr >=500 but not on dialysis; 8% if immediate dialysis needed.
