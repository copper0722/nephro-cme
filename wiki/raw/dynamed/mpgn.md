# Membranoproliferative Glomerulonephritis (MPGN) (DynaMed)

Updated 03 Dec 2025. 46 pages.

## Overview
MPGN is a histologic PATTERN (not single disease) caused by deposition of immune complexes and/or complement in mesangium and capillary walls. Mesangial proliferation + capillary wall remodeling. Typically children and young adults. Incidence 0.14-0.93/100K (decreasing). 7-10% of biopsy-proven GN. Two major types: immune complex-mediated (classical pathway) and C3 glomerulopathy (complement-mediated, alternative pathway).

## Contemporary Classification
Immune complex-mediated: deposits of both Ig and complement; caused by chronic antigenemia (HCV, HBV, autoimmune, malignancy, monoclonal gammopathy, infections). C3 glomerulopathy: complement deposits only (C3 dominant, no Ig); caused by genetic mutations (CFH, CFI, CFB, C3, CFHR) or acquired (C3 nephritic factor) alternative pathway dysregulation; subdivided into C3 GN and dense deposit disease (DDD). Historical type I/II/III classification based on EM deposit location is OBSOLETE.

## Causes by IF Category
Immune complex: HCV (with mixed cryoglobulinemia), HBV, HIV, parvovirus B19; SLE, Sjogren, RA; endocarditis, shunt nephritis, visceral abscess; multiple myeloma, B cell lymphoma, Waldenstrom, CLL; fibrillary GN. C3 glomerulopathy: hereditary (C3, CFB, CFH, CFI, MCP/CD46, CFHR1-5 mutations) or acquired (C3 nephritic factor 50-80%, C4/C5 nephritic factor, factor H/B autoantibodies). Ig/complement-negative: TMA healing, APS, sickle cell.

## Clinical Presentation
Nephrotic syndrome 40-70%; nephritic syndrome 20-30%; asymptomatic proteinuria/hematuria 20-30%; recurrent gross hematuria 10-20%. HTN in >1/3. Kidney dysfunction in ~50%. Hypocomplementemia (low C3 +/- C4, may be intermittent). Drusen on retinal exam may indicate DDD/C3 glomerulopathy.

## Diagnosis
Biopsy required. LM: diffuse mesangial proliferation + capillary wall thickening ("tram-track" on silver/PAS); lobular appearance; crescents in ~10%. IF critical for classification: Ig+C3 = immune complex; C3 dominant = C3 glomerulopathy. EM: subendothelial (immune complex); intramembranous dense (DDD). All patients evaluated for secondary causes. Complement workup if C3 glomerulopathy suspected: CH50, AP50, C3/C4, factor H, complement genetic testing.

## Treatment
Immune complex MPGN: treat underlying cause first (HCV: DAA [KDIGO Level 1, Grade A]; HBV: antiviral; autoimmune: disease-specific). Idiopathic immune complex MPGN: RAAS blockade; if progressive, immunosuppression (steroids +/- MMF or CYC based on severity). C3 glomerulopathy: MMF + corticosteroid first-line; eculizumab if no response; pegcetacoplan (Empaveli) and iptacopan (Fabhalta) received expanded FDA approval. Rapidly progressive crescentic MPGN: pulse steroids + CYC (similar to ANCA vasculitis). All: supportive care (ACEi/ARB, Na restriction, diuretics, statin, anticoagulation if needed).
