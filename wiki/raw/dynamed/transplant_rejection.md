# Renal Transplant Rejection (DynaMed)

## Definition
- Immune response against donor graft alloantigens → antibody-mediated (ABMR) or T cell-mediated (TCMR) rejection
- Incidence: < 15% for acute rejection within 1 year; prevalence <= 12%
- Acute ABMR: 5-7% overall; 55% in presensitized patients

## Classification
### By Timing
- Hyperacute: minutes-hours post-transplant (rare with modern crossmatching)
- Acute (early): days to months
- Chronic (late): months to years

### By Mechanism
- ABMR (antibody/humoral/B-cell mediated): hyperacute, acute, chronic
- TCMR (T cell/cellular mediated): acute, chronic
- Mixed rejection: both ABMR + TCMR features
- Subclinical: histological changes on protocol biopsy without clinical dysfunction (TCMR 13%, ABMR 14%)
- Borderline: histological changes suspicious for rejection (Banff)

## Banff 2015 Classification Criteria
### ABMR requires ALL of:
1. Histological evidence of acute or chronic tissue injury
2. Current/recent interaction between vascular endothelium and antibodies (C4d, microvascular inflammation)
3. Donor-specific antibodies (DSA)
- C4d-negative rejection: microvascular injury (glomerulitis, peritubular capillaritis, TMA) in presence of DSA

### TCMR requires:
- Tubulitis with or without intimal arteritis
- Acute: T-cell infiltrates + parenchymal injury on biopsy
- Chronic: progressive decline + invasion by T cells/macrophages of renal parenchyma

## Risk Factors
### Recipient
- Younger age (28% rejection in 18-29y vs 14% in > 70y)
- African American ethnicity (14.1% vs 7.5% at 1 year; CYP3A5 polymorphism → lower tacrolimus exposure)
- Nonadherence (23-50% of recipients): psychiatric illness, substance abuse, poor social support, complex regimen
- Prior transplant history
- Morbid obesity (BMI >= 35: OR 1.56 pre-discharge)
- Presensitization (preformed DSA)

### Donor
- Older age
- Deceased > living donor
- HLA mismatch (especially HLA-DQ mismatch: 25.1% vs 15.6% any rejection)
- Brain/cardiac death (vs living)

### Immunological
- Preformed DSA detected by solid phase assay: RR 1.98 for ABMR
- DSA with C1q binding capacity: 48% ABMR vs 16% without C1q (NEJM 2013)
- De novo DSA: 15% develop by mean 4.6 years; 10-year graft survival 57% vs 98%
- HLA mismatch number (RR 1.18 per mismatch)

### Other
- CMV infection, delayed graft function, longer cold ischemia time, ischemia-reperfusion injury

## Pathogenesis
### ABMR
- Hyperacute: preformed anti-HLA antibodies → complement cascade → endothelial necrosis, platelet deposition, local coagulation
- Acute: DSA bind endothelial MHC antigens → complement activation → C4d deposition, inflammation, microvascular injury
- Chronic: slow complement activation → cellular hypertrophy, GBM duplication, transplant glomerulopathy

### TCMR
- Donor antigens presented via MHC → T cell activation → cytokine release → interstitial infiltration → tubulitis
- CD4/CD8 T cells directly contact tubular cells → apoptosis
- Chronic: persistent T cell invasion → interstitial fibrosis, tubular atrophy

## Clinical Presentation
- Hyperacute: graft becomes flaccid/mottled/cyanotic intraoperatively after cross-clamp release
- Acute/chronic: often asymptomatic; detected by rising SCr, decreased eGFR, new proteinuria
- Less common: sudden UO reduction, graft tenderness, fever

## Diagnosis
- Monitor SCr [Strong], proteinuria/albuminuria [Conditional], US [Conditional]
- Biopsy with Banff classification for confirmation [Strong for persistent unexplained SCr rise]
- Suspect if graft dysfunction despite excluding: volume depletion, CNI toxicity, ureteric obstruction, vascular impairment, infection

## Differential Diagnosis by Timing
- First week: hyperacute rejection, renal artery/vein thrombosis, delayed graft function (ATN)
- < 6 months: urinary leak, collecting system obstruction, BKV, CNI toxicity, major illness-associated AKI
- >= 6 months: BKV, transplant renal artery stenosis, CNI toxicity, recurrent/de novo GN, PTLD, dehydration, HTN, sepsis, drugs (ACEi/ARB/NSAID)

## kSORT (Kidney Solid Organ Response Test)
- 17-gene blood expression panel; validation: sensitivity 91%, specificity 99%, PPV 95%, NPV 98%
