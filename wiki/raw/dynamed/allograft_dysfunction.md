# Renal Allograft Dysfunction and Failure (DynaMed)

## Definition
- Graft failure: failure of graft function requiring RRT or retransplantation
- Chronic allograft injury (KDIGO): progressive decline not due to recurrent disease or other known causes; interstitial fibrosis or tubular atrophy
- Delayed graft function: need for dialysis in first week after transplant
- Long-term graft failure: ~4% per year
- Chronic allograft injury: ~25% at 1 year, ~90% at 10 years post-transplant

## Classifications
- Acute vs chronic
- Early (< 6 months) vs late (> 6 months)
- Immune vs nonimmune
- Prerenal vs intrinsic vs postrenal
- Reversible vs nonreversible

## Epidemiology
- 5-year deceased donor graft survival: 67.7% (2002) → 75.1% (2017)
- 5-year living donor graft survival: 76.5% (2002) → 84.7% (2017)
- Allograft failure at 9 years: 22% (France, 2004-2014)
- Early allograft failure (< 30 days): decreased from 7% (1990) to < 1% (2009)
- 12.5% of waitlist additions = patients needing retransplant

## Risk Factors
### Donor
- Deceased donor, KDPI > 85%, post cardiac death, older age, hypertension, renal impairment
- Female sex (smaller kidneys → fewer nephrons)

### Recipient
- Female sex, pediatric, size mismatch
- Genetic differences in immunosuppressive metabolism
- Obesity, hypertension, dyslipidemia, smoking, proteinuria
- Pre/post transplant diabetes
- Nonadherence (23-50%): risk factors include adolescence, psychiatric illness, substance abuse, complex regimen, cost, poor access
- De novo DSA: 15% develop; graft failure 13% vs 2.9% without (p < 0.01)

### Immunological
- ABO incompatibility, HLA mismatch
- Preformed DSA (SPA): RR 1.76 for graft failure; RR 1.98 for ABMR
- Anti-HLA with C1q binding: 5-year graft survival 54% vs 93% (without C1q) vs 94% (no DSA) (NEJM 2013)
- Interstitial fibrosis + tubular atrophy: HR 2.22
- Transplant glomerulopathy: HR 2.26

### Other
- CMV infection, longer cold ischemia, delayed graft function (OR 3.7), ischemia-reperfusion injury

## Causes by Timing
### Within First Week
- Hyperacute rejection
- Renal artery/vein thrombosis (1-3% of grafts; high allograft loss rate)
- Delayed allograft function (most common cause: ATN from ischemia-reperfusion)

### Early (< 6 months)
- Urinary leak, collecting system obstruction
- BK polyomavirus nephropathy
- CNI toxicity
- Acute rejection
- AKI from major illness

### Late (>= 6 months)
- BK virus, transplant renal artery stenosis, CNI toxicity
- Recurrent or de novo GN
- PTLD, chronic rejection (ABMR most common)
- Dehydration, HTN, sepsis, drug effects

## Causes by Etiology
### Prerenal
- Volume depletion (blood loss, vomiting/diarrhea, osmotic diuresis)
- Systemic vasodilation (sepsis)
- Intrarenal vasoconstriction (CNIs, cyclosporine → afferent arteriolar constriction)
- Transplant renal artery stenosis (2.4-6.5%)
- Hypercalcemia

### Intrinsic (Renal)
- ATN (ischemia-reperfusion, nephrotoxins)
- BK virus nephropathy: first year, asymptomatic, viral load > 10,000 copies/mL; biopsy = intranuclear inclusions; treatment = reduce immunosuppression, low-dose cidofovir, switch MMF to leflunomide/azathioprine
- TMA: CNI toxicity, recurrent TTP/aHUS, antibody-mediated rejection
- Medication-associated: CNI nephrotoxicity (isometric vacuolization on biopsy), other nephrotoxins
- Recurrent/de novo GN (36.6% of death-censored graft failures)
- Acute/chronic rejection

### Postrenal
- Urinary leak: first weeks; Cr in drain fluid > blood Cr; confirmed by radionuclide imaging/cystogram
- Ureteric obstruction (2.4-6.5%): edema, clots, stones, strictures, lymphocele, hematoma

### Vascular
- Renal artery thrombosis: sudden cessation of UO, painless; US + radionuclide; treatment = urgent thrombectomy (salvage rare → nephrectomy usually required)
- Renal vein thrombosis: pain over allograft + vascular engorgement; treatment = thrombectomy (better prognosis than arterial)
- Transplant renal artery stenosis: months-years post-transplant; suspect if refractory HTN/declining GFR

## Evaluation
- Monitor SCr [Strong], eGFR [Conditional], proteinuria [Conditional], BP
- If declining GFR/proteinuria: measure CNI blood levels → if at target, perform transplant imaging → biopsy
- Biopsy: for persistent unexplained SCr rise [Strong]; for all patients with declining function to determine cause of chronic allograft injury [Strong]; after acute rejection if SCr does not return to baseline [Conditional]

## Most Common Causes of Return to Dialysis
- Chronic allograft injury (HTN, CNI toxicity, chronic ABMR)
- Recurrent primary kidney disease

## Causes of Graft Failure (Cohort of 1,317 patients, mean F/U 50 months)
- 330 graft failures (25%): death with function 41.8%, primary nonfunction 11.8%, graft failure censored for death 46.3%
- Among 153 death-censored failures: glomerular 36.6%, fibrosis/atrophy 30.7%, medical/surgical 16.3%, acute rejection 11.8%
