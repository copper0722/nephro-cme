# Hypertension Management in Secondary Stroke Prevention

## Overview
Management of BP in patients with a history of stroke (especially **hemorrhagic stroke**) focuses on aggressive target achievement to prevent recurrence. The transition from acute inpatient management (short-acting agents) to chronic outpatient management (long-acting agents) is critical to avoid **BP lability**.

## Exam Logic
*   **BP Targets:** Current guidelines (ACC/AHA) recommend **< 130/80 mmHg** for secondary stroke prevention. Lowering SBP specifically reduces the risk of recurrent **hemorrhagic stroke**.
*   **The "Hospital-to-Home" Pitfall:** Patients are often discharged on short-acting agents (e.g., **clonidine, hydralazine**) used for acute titration. In an outpatient setting, these are **suboptimal** because:
    *   **Short half-life (t½):** Leads to significant BP fluctuations (lability).
    *   **Adherence:** Multiple daily doses ↓ compliance.
    *   **Side effects:** Clonidine may cause excessive sedation/drowsiness.
*   **Drug Selection Logic:**
    *   **RAS Inhibitors (ACEi/ARB):** First-line for stroke prevention.
    *   **Thiazide-type Diuretics:** **Chlorthalidone** is preferred over hydrochlorothiazide due to its **longer t½**, providing more stable 24-hour BP control and reducing the risk of "spikes."
    *   **Combination Therapy:** RASi + Thiazide-type diuretic is a potent combination for secondary prevention.
*   **Secondary HTN Screening:** Any patient presenting with a **hypertensive emergency** (e.g., basal ganglia hemorrhage) despite previous control warrants a full workup for secondary causes (Renovascular, Primary Aldosteronism, Pheochromocytoma, OSA).

## Clinical Pearls for the Exam
*   **BP Lability:** Defined as high variability in BP readings. It is associated with ↑ cardiovascular risk and is exacerbated by short-acting medications.
*   **Measurement Protocol:** Standardized, unattended automated office BP (AOBP) is more predictive of outcomes than routine manual office BP.
*   **Medication Transition:** When weaning short-acting agents (like clonidine), do so **gradually** to avoid rebound hypertension.
