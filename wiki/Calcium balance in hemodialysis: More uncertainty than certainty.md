# Dialysate Calcium (DCa) and Calcium Balance in Hemodialysis

## Overview
Calcium balance in HD is the net result of oral intake, intestinal absorption, and the bidirectional flux between the patient and the dialysate. Unlike serum calcium, **calcium balance** refers to total body stores and is not directly reflected by serum levels.

### Calcium Fractions and Dialyzability
*   **Diffusible/Ultrafilterable (~60%):** Ionized calcium and nonionized complexed calcium (e.g., bound to phosphate, bicarbonate). These are the only fractions affected by HD.
*   **Non-diffusible:** Protein-bound calcium (mainly albumin). This fraction does **not** cross the dialysis membrane.

### DCa Concentration and Net Balance
*   **DCa 3.5 mEq/L:** Consistently **positive** calcium balance.
*   **DCa 2.5 mEq/L:** Typically **negative or neutral** balance.
*   **DCa 3.0 mEq/L:** Variable; can be positive or negative depending on the patient.
*   **DCa 2.75 mEq/L:** Emerging as a potential "sweet spot" (mildly positive balance, stable PTH).

## Exam Logic: High-Yield Concepts

### 1. The DCa $\rightarrow$ PTH Axis
*   **High DCa (>3.0 mEq/L):** ↑ Serum Ca $\rightarrow$ ↓ PTH $\rightarrow$ ↓ Need for Vitamin D analogues.
*   **Low DCa (<3.0 mEq/L):** ↓ Serum Ca $\rightarrow$ ↑ PTH $\rightarrow$ ↑ Need for Vitamin D analogues.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not confuse **serum calcium** with **calcium balance**. A patient can have normal serum calcium but be in a chronic negative calcium balance (depleting bone stores).

### 2. Modality-Specific Requirements
*   **Extended HD (Daily/Nocturnal):** Increased bone mineral loss $\rightarrow$ Requires **higher DCa** (typically 3.0 mEq/L) to prevent hypocalcemia and SHPT.
*   **Online Hemodiafiltration (OHDF):**
    *   **Post-dilution:** More positive calcium balance (similar to or higher than conventional HD).
    *   **Pre-dilution:** Higher convective loss of calcium $\rightarrow$ Often **negative balance**.
    *   **Clinical Pearl:** When switching from HD $\rightarrow$ Pre-dilution OHDF, consider **increasing DCa by ~0.5 mEq/L**.

### 3. Calcium Profiling
*   **Strategy:** Start with low DCa, increase to high DCa (e.g., 2.5 $\rightarrow$ 3.5 mEq/L) during the last 2 hours.
*   **Goal:** Reduce **intradialytic hypotension** and stabilize QTc interval while avoiding the long-term risks of a constant high-calcium load (vascular calcification).

## Key Trials & Evidence
*   **Kirmizis et al. (2020):** Review of DCa concentrations. Bottom Line: DCa choice must be individualized based on oral intake, binder type, and modality (Pre- vs Post-dilution HDF).
*   **General Consensus (KDOQI/KDIGO):** Suggests a range of 2.5–3.0 mEq/L, but emphasizes limiting calcium-based binders in patients with arterial calcification or low PTH.
*   **Kyriazis et al.:** Demonstrated that **calcium profiling** (increasing DCa late in the session) reduces hypotensive episodes.
