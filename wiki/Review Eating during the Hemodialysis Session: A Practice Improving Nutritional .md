# Intradialytic Nutrition & Hemodynamic Stability

## Overview
Controversy exists regarding **intradialytic feeding** (meals/supplements during HD). While some guidelines focus on improving **Protein-Energy Wasting (PEW)** and caloric intake, others warn against hemodynamic instability and reduced dialysis adequacy.

## 1. Clinical Conflict: Guidelines & Logic
*   **Pro-Feeding Logic (e.g., ISRNM 2018):** Focuses on nutritional status. Intradialytic supplementation $\rightarrow$ $\uparrow$ caloric intake $\rightarrow$ $\downarrow$ PEW, $\downarrow$ inflammatory markers, $\uparrow$ HR-QoL.
*   **Anti-Feeding Logic (e.g., EBPG 2007):** Focuses on hemodynamic safety. Postprandial state $\rightarrow$ $\downarrow$ BP $\rightarrow$ $\uparrow$ **Intradialytic Hypotension (IDH)**.

## 2. Intradialytic Hypotension (IDH)
### Pathophysiology
*   **Postprandial Redistribution:** Oral intake $\rightarrow$ $\uparrow$ blood flow to gastrointestinal (splanchnic) circulation $\rightarrow$ $\downarrow$ effective circulating volume $\rightarrow$ $\downarrow$ systemic BP.
*   **Synergy with UF:** This physiological drop is exacerbated by concurrent **ultrafiltration (UF)**, increasing the risk of symptomatic IDH.

### Definitions & Risks (Exam Logic)
*   **EBPG Definition:** $\downarrow$ SBP $\geq 20$ mmHg or $\downarrow$ MAP $\geq 10$ mmHg + symptoms/interventions.
*   **Clinical Significance:** IDH is not benign. Associated with:
    *   $\uparrow$ Vascular access thrombosis.
    *   $\uparrow$ Loss of residual renal function.
    *   $\uparrow$ CV events and all-cause mortality.

### Evidence Summary
*   **Observational:** Higher caloric/fluid intake during HD $\rightarrow$ $\uparrow$ likelihood of hypotension and $\uparrow$ need for interventions (e.g., mannitol).
*   **Interventional:** Consistent findings across multiple studies (Sherman, Zoccali, Borzou) show a significantly higher rate of BP decline and symptomatic IDH in "fed" vs. "fasting" sessions.
*   **Timing:** Postprandial BP drop occurs regardless of whether the meal is given 1 or 2 hours after HD initiation.

## 3. Dialysis Adequacy
*   **Mechanism of Interference:** Postprandial redistribution of blood flow to the gut $\rightarrow$ $\downarrow$ blood supply to skeletal muscle/other tissues $\rightarrow$ potential reduction in the delivered dose of dialysis (adequacy).
