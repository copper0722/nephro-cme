# Paraprotein-Associated Kidney Disease (MGRS + Myeloma Kidney)

## Exam Logic Summary

Paraprotein renal disease tests: (1) distinguishing MGRS from myeloma cast nephropathy, (2) recognizing histologic patterns (organized vs non-organized deposits), (3) understanding that MGRS requires clone-directed therapy despite NOT meeting myeloma criteria, (4) differentiating cast nephropathy (selective FLC proteinuria, bland sediment) from AL amyloidosis (nonselective proteinuria, Congo red+) from LCDD (nodular GN, linear IF). Key distractor: assuming small M-spike = benign (MGRS proves otherwise).

**Textbook ref:** Brenner Ch. 30 (Dysproteinemias), Harrison Ch. 107 (Plasma Cell Disorders)

---

## Definitions

### MGRS (Monoclonal Gammopathy of Renal Significance)

- Renal injury from monoclonal immunoglobulin (MIg) secreted by B-cell/plasma cell clone that does NOT meet criteria for overt myeloma or lymphoma
- Term introduced 2012 to distinguish from MGUS (no organ damage)
- IMWG criteria: M protein-associated renal disease + bone marrow plasma cells < 10% + M protein < 3 g/dL (may be undetectable) + no myeloma-defining events + no lymphoma evidence
- Clone undetectable in approximately 38% of cases
- Treatment targets the clone (not just supportive care) to prevent renal deterioration

### Multiple Myeloma

- Bone marrow clonal plasma cells >= 10% OR biopsy-proven plasmacytoma
- Plus >= 1 myeloma-defining event: CRAB criteria or biomarkers of malignancy
  - **C**alcium > 0.25 mmol/L above normal or > 2.75 mmol/L (> 11 mg/dL)
  - **R**enal: creatinine clearance < 40 mL/min or serum Cr > 177 mcmol/L (> 2 mg/dL)
  - **A**nemia: Hb > 2 g/dL below normal or < 10 g/dL
  - **B**one: >= 1 osteolytic lesion on imaging
- Biomarkers: plasma cells >= 60%, FLC ratio >= 100 (involved >= 100 mg/L), > 1 focal lesion >= 5 mm on MRI
- Renal impairment in 20%-50% at diagnosis; most common malignancy causing ESKD

## Classification: Organized vs Non-Organized Deposits

| Category | Subcategory | Diseases |
|---|---|---|
| **Organized deposits** | Fibrillar | AL amyloidosis, AH amyloidosis, AHL amyloidosis, monoclonal fibrillary GN |
| | Microtubular | Immunotactoid glomerulopathy, type I cryoglobulinemic GN |
| | Tubular (crystals) | Light chain Fanconi syndrome, crystal storing histiocytosis |
| | Tubular (no crystals) | Proximal tubulopathy without crystals |
| **Non-organized deposits** | Glomerular | LCDD, HCDD, LHCDD, PGNMID, C3 glomerulopathy with monoclonal gammopathy |
| | Vascular | Thrombotic microangiopathy |

**Exam pearl:** >= 10 histological patterns exist in MGRS. The exam typically tests AL amyloidosis, LCDD, cast nephropathy, and immunotactoid glomerulopathy. The key differentiator is IF pattern (monotypic kappa or lambda restriction) + EM ultrastructure.

## Cast Nephropathy (Myeloma Kidney)

- Most common cause of renal injury in myeloma (up to 90% of paraprotein-dependent disease)
- Pathogenesis: FLC overproduction exceeds proximal tubule megalin-cubilin reabsorptive capacity -> FLCs reach distal tubule -> bind Tamm-Horsfall protein -> form obstructing casts
- Cast formation promoted by: volume depletion, hypercalcemia, furosemide, nephrotoxic drugs, acidic pH, high FLC concentration

### Diagnostic Clues

| Feature | Cast nephropathy | AL amyloidosis | LCDD |
|---|---|---|---|
| Proteinuria type | Selective FLC (albumin < 10% of urinary protein) | Nonselective (albuminuria) | Nonselective |
| Serum FLC | > 500-1,500 mg/L | < 500 mg/L | Variable |
| Urine albumin excretion | < 25% of total protein | Significant | Significant |
| Congo red | Negative | Positive (apple-green birefringence) | Negative |
| IF | Not diagnostic (casts show light chain) | Lambda predominant | Kappa predominant, linear along TBM/GBM |
| LM | Fractured casts in distal tubules | Amorphous deposits | Nodular GN (Kimmelstiel-Wilson-like) |
| EM | N/A | 8-12 nm fibrils, random arrangement | Granular deposits along BM |
| Organ involvement | Kidney only | Heart, liver, nerve, kidney | Heart, liver, skin (common but asymptomatic) |
| Clinical | AKI, bland sediment, anemia | Nephrotic syndrome, hypotension, cardiomyopathy | Nephrotic syndrome, HTN |

**Exam pearl:** Cast nephropathy = selective proteinuria (light chains only, albumin < 10%). If you see significant albuminuria in a myeloma patient, think amyloidosis or LCDD, NOT cast nephropathy. Urine dipstick is NEGATIVE in cast nephropathy (detects albumin, not light chains).

## Management

### Cast Nephropathy (Myeloma)

- **Urgent antimyeloma therapy** with bortezomib-based regimens (Strong Recommendation)
- Hydration >= 3 L/day (Strong Recommendation)
- High-dose steroids during initial phase
- Consider plasma exchange or high-cutoff hemodialysis for acute renal impairment (Conditional Recommendation)
- AVOID: NSAIDs, contrast, nephrotoxic agents, volume depletion
- Bortezomib: no dose adjustment needed in renal impairment
- If reduced renal function: cyclophosphamide preferred over melphalan; thalidomide preferred over lenalidomide

### MGRS

- Clone-directed therapy (targeting the underlying B-cell or plasma cell clone)
- Choose chemotherapy based on: clone type (B-cell vs plasma cell), renal function
- Bortezomib: no dose adjustment, can be used in ESKD
- If plasma cell clone: bortezomib-based regimens
- If B-cell clone: rituximab-based regimens
- Monitor hematological + renal parameters during treatment
- Kidney transplantation: consider after achieving hematologic response (risk of recurrence in graft)

**Exam pearl:** The critical concept of MGRS is that the clone is "small" (does not meet myeloma criteria) but the immunoglobulin it produces is NEPHROTOXIC. Treatment targets the clone, not the kidney. This distinguishes MGRS from MGUS: MGUS needs monitoring only, MGRS needs treatment.

## Free Light Chain (FLC) Physiology

- Polyclonal FLCs: 500 mg/day produced, freely filtered by glomerulus, reabsorbed by megalin-cubilin in PCT
- Only 1-10 mg normally in urine (Bence Jones proteins = monoclonal FLCs in urine)
- Serum FLC assay: kappa:lambda ratio
  - Normal renal function: 0.26-1.65
  - Renal impairment: 0.37-3.17 (both polyclonally elevated, but ratio remains normal unless clonal)
- Abnormal ratio = clonal process
- FLC half-life: 2-6 hours (rapid renal clearance)
- 30-40 fold increase in polyclonal FLC with renal impairment or polyclonal Ig production

## IMWG Classification of Plasma Cell Disorders (Exam Table)

| Disorder | Key criteria |
|---|---|
| Non-IgM MGUS | M protein < 3 g/dL, BM plasma cells < 10%, no CRAB |
| IgM MGUS | IgM < 3 g/dL, BM lymphoplasmacytic < 10%, no lymphoproliferative signs |
| Light chain MGUS | Abnormal FLC ratio, no heavy chain on IFx, BM < 10%, urinary MC protein < 500 mg/24h |
| Smoldering myeloma | IgG/IgA >= 3 g/dL OR urinary MC >= 500 mg/24h OR BM 10%-60%, NO amyloidosis/myeloma-defining events |
| MGRS | BM < 10%, M protein-associated renal disease, no myeloma-defining events |
| Multiple myeloma | BM >= 10% + >= 1 myeloma-defining event (CRAB or biomarker) |
| POEMS | Polyneuropathy + monoclonal plasma cell disorder (lambda) + >= 1 major + >= 1 minor criteria |
| AL amyloidosis | Amyloid-related systemic syndrome + Congo red positive + evidence of clonal plasma cell disorder |

## High-Yield Exam Scenarios

1. **Elderly patient with AKI, anemia, bone pain, elevated Cr, urine dipstick negative for protein but sulfosalicylic acid test positive:** Cast nephropathy (myeloma kidney). Dipstick detects albumin; SSA detects all proteins including light chains. Check SPEP/UPEP, serum FLC.

2. **Patient with nephrotic syndrome, low BP, periorbital purpura, macroglossia, restrictive cardiomyopathy:** AL amyloidosis. Congo red + fat pad biopsy. Lambda light chain predominant. Cardiac involvement = worst prognosis.

3. **Biopsy shows nodular GN resembling diabetic nephropathy but patient is NOT diabetic; linear IF staining along GBM/TBM (kappa):** LCDD (light chain deposition disease). Kappa predominant (unlike AL which is lambda predominant).

4. **Patient with monoclonal IgG3, MPGN pattern on biopsy, C3/C1q deposits, hematuria:** Proliferative GN with monoclonal Ig deposits (PGNMID). Mostly IgG3 subclass. Non-organized deposits.

5. **Patient with renal impairment, small M-spike < 1 g/dL, BM plasma cells 5%, kidney biopsy shows light chain deposits:** MGRS. Despite not meeting myeloma criteria, clone-directed therapy is needed because the immunoglobulin is directly causing kidney injury.

6. **Myeloma patient with AKI: how to differentiate cast nephropathy from other causes:** Selective FLC proteinuria + FLC > 500-1,500 mg/L = cast nephropathy. Nonselective proteinuria + FLC < 500 mg/L + extrarenal involvement = amyloidosis or LCDD. Always consider paraprotein-independent causes: hypercalcemia, volume depletion, nephrotoxins (25% of biopsied myeloma patients had non-paraprotein lesions).
