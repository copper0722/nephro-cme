# Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) in HIV-Infected Patients

## Exam Logic
The core of this topic for the TSN exam revolves around **screening frequency**, **referral triggers**, and **drug-induced nephrotoxicity** (specifically Tenofovir). 

*   **The "Tenofovir Trap":** Exams often test the threshold for switching Tenofovir. The trigger is a **$\downarrow$ GFR $> 25\%$ from baseline** AND an absolute **GFR $< 60$ mL/min/1.73m²**. 
*   **Referral Thresholds:** Note the specific numbers. A GFR $< 30$ is an automatic referral, but a GFR between 30–60 requires a **$> 25\%$ drop** or **albuminuria $> 300$ mg/day** to trigger a nephrology consult.
*   **HIVAN vs. General CKD:** HIV-associated nephropathy (HIVAN) is highly responsive to **ART**. The combination of **ART + ACEi/ARB** is the gold standard. Steroids are a "weak" adjunct—do not prioritize them over ART.
*   **BP Targets:** Distinguish between the two targets based on albuminuria:
    *   Mild/None $\rightarrow < 140/90$ mmHg.
    *   Moderate/Severe $\rightarrow < 130/80$ mmHg.

## Clinical Management Summary

### 1. Screening & Monitoring
*   **GFR (Creatinine-based):** At ART initiation/change and $\ge 2\times$ yearly in stable patients.
*   **Albuminuria/UA:** At baseline, ART initiation/change, and $\ge 1\times$ yearly.

### 2. Nephrology Referral Criteria
Referral is indicated if:
*   **GFR decline $> 25\%$** from baseline AND **GFR $< 60$ mL/min/1.73m²** (after removing nephrotoxins).
*   **Albuminuria $> 300$ mg/day**.
*   **Hematuria** combined with $\uparrow$ BP or proteinuria.
*   **Advanced CKD:** GFR $< 30$ mL/min/1.73m².

### 3. Pharmacologic Interventions
*   **ART:** Mandatory for all, including ESRD (reduces mortality). Essential in **HIVAN** to prevent progression to ESRD.
*   **Tenofovir (TDF):** Avoid if GFR $< 60$ mL/min/1.73m².
*   **ACEi/ARBs:** Indicated for HIVAN or significant albuminuria ($> 30$ mg/day in DM; $> 300$ mg/day in non-DM).
*   **Statins:** Based on CV risk ($\ge 7.5\%$ 10-year risk).
*   **Aspirin:** 75–100 mg/day (balance vs. bleeding risk).

### 4. HIVAN Specifics
*   **First-line:** ART + ACEi/ARB.
*   **Adjunct:** Corticosteroids (considered for biopsy-confirmed HIVAN, though evidence is weak).

*   **IDSA/HIVMA Guidelines (2014):** (Systematic Review/Expert Panel) $\rightarrow$ Bottom Line: Established the GFR $< 60$ and $> 25\%$ drop threshold for Tenofovir cessation and defined the $130/80$ vs $140/90$ BP targets.
*   **General HIVAN Cohorts:** (Observational) $\rightarrow$ Bottom Line: ART significantly reduces the risk of progression to ESRD in patients with biopsy-proven HIVAN.
