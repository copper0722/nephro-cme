# Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease (ADPKD)

## Exam Logic Summary

ADPKD is a classic genetics/nephrology crossover topic. The exam tests: (1) PKD1 vs PKD2 genotype-phenotype correlation, (2) diagnostic ultrasound criteria by age, (3) tolvaptan indications and Mayo classification for rapid progression, (4) HALT-PKD BP targets, (5) intracranial aneurysm screening criteria, (6) extrarenal manifestations. Common distractor: recommending routine genetic testing (not indicated unless atypical presentation or donor evaluation).

**Textbook ref:** Brenner Ch. 43 (Cystic Diseases), KDIGO 2015 ADPKD Controversies Conference, SWGIKD guidelines

---

## Definition and Epidemiology

- Most common hereditary kidney disease; most common genetic cause of ESKD
- Prevalence: 1 in 1,000-2,500 (12.5 million affected worldwide)
- Fourth most common cause of RRT worldwide
- ADPKD accounts for 7-10% of all ESKD
- ESRD incidence: 7.9/million (US), 3.9-15.3/million (Europe), 4.8/million (Japan)
- 50% chance of inheritance (autosomal dominant)
- 10-15% are de novo mutations (no family history)

## Genetics (MUST MEMORIZE)

| Gene | Chromosome | Protein | Frequency | Mean ESRD Age | Severity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PKD1** | 16p13.3 | Polycystin-1 | 78% | 58 years | More severe |
| **PKD2** | 4q21 | Polycystin-2 | 15% | 79 years | Less severe |
| De novo | Either | -- | 10-15% | Variable | Variable |

- PKD1 and PKD2 encode integral membrane proteins polycystin-1 and polycystin-2
- Mutations cause cAMP dysregulation → cystogenesis
- Two-hit hypothesis: germline mutation + somatic second hit → cyst formation
- > 1,500 mutations known in PKD1 and PKD2

**Exam pearl:** PKD1 = MORE SEVERE disease, EARLIER ESRD (mean 58 vs 79 years). If a question asks about a young patient with rapid CKD progression and PKD → think PKD1. PKD2 patients often diagnosed later in life.

## Pathophysiology

- Renal function initially preserved by compensatory hyperfiltration (decades)
- Hypertension develops early (> 60% before detectable GFR loss) due to:
  - ↑ Plasma renin and aldosterone
  - Intrarenal ischemia from cyst compression
  - Impaired tubular sodium handling
  - Endothelial dysfunction
  - Extracellular volume expansion
- GFR typically remains normal for decades, then declines rapidly once critical mass of cysts disrupts parenchyma
- Cysts grow ~5% per year; total kidney volume (TKV) is the best predictor of renal decline

---

## Clinical Presentation

- May be asymptomatic for decades (incidental finding on imaging)
- Pain: most common symptom (71% low back, 61% abdomen/flank)
  - Acute: cyst hemorrhage, infection, nephrolithiasis
  - Chronic: cyst expansion, mechanical compression
- Hypertension: most common manifestation, often earliest sign
- Gross hematuria: from cyst hemorrhage, usually self-limiting (2-7 days)
- Symptoms of impaired renal function in later stages

## Diagnosis

### Ultrasound Criteria (KDIGO Unified Criteria for PKD1 or Unknown Genotype)

| Age | Diagnosis Requires |
|---|---|
| 15-29 years | >= 3 unilateral or bilateral cysts |
| 30-39 years | >= 3 unilateral or bilateral cysts |
| 40-59 years | >= 2 unilateral or bilateral cysts |

- Absence of renal cysts aged 15-59: negative predictive value 90-100% → sufficient to rule out disease
- Ultrasound not optimal for ruling out in patients < 40 years (lower sensitivity) → consider MRI
- Without family history: bilateral renal enlargement + cysts + hepatic cysts

### MRI

- More sensitive than ultrasound (detects cysts 2-3 mm)
- Age 16-40 with family history: > 10 cysts on MRI = indicative; < 5 cysts = sufficient to exclude
- Preferred for measuring TKV (used for Mayo classification and tolvaptan eligibility)
- MRA without gadolinium for intracranial aneurysm screening

### Genetic Testing

- Routine genetic testing NOT suggested
- Indications:
  - Patients < 2 years with bilateral cysts (early/very early onset)
  - Atypical imaging or extrarenal features suggesting other cystic disease
  - Potential living kidney donors from affected families
  - Prenatal/preimplantation genetic diagnosis
  - Definitive diagnosis in young potential donors [SWGIKD Grade D]
- NGS: sensitivity 99.2%, specificity 99.9% for ADPKD diagnosis
- Genetic testing yields diagnosis in ~90% of tested patients

## Mayo Imaging Classification (for Tolvaptan Eligibility)

Uses height-adjusted total kidney volume (htTKV) from MRI or CT, plotted against age:

| Class | htTKV Growth Rate | Estimated GFR Decline | Tolvaptan Candidate? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1A | < 1.5%/year | Slow | Unlikely to benefit |
| 1B | 1.5-3%/year | Moderate | Consider if young |
| **1C** | 3-4.5%/year | Rapid | **Yes -- likely benefit** |
| **1D** | 4.5-6%/year | Very rapid | **Yes -- clear benefit** |
| **1E** | > 6%/year | Extremely rapid | **Yes -- strongest indication** |

**Exam pearl:** Mayo 1C-1E = rapidly progressing ADPKD = tolvaptan candidates. Class 2 (atypical cysts, not typical bilateral diffuse enlargement) are NOT classified by this system and need alternative assessment.

## Management

### Blood Pressure

- In ADPKD patients < 50 years without CKD and GFR >= 60: target BP < 110/75 mmHg (HALT-PKD Study A)
- In ADPKD patients with CKD: target BP < 130/80 mmHg (standard CKD target)
- ACEi or ARB preferred (RAAS blockade slows cyst growth)

### HALT-PKD Trials

| Study | Population | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **HALT-PKD A** | eGFR > 60, young | Low BP target (95-110/60-75) + ACEi/ARB → 14% slower TKV growth vs standard BP; but no GFR benefit at 5 years |
| **HALT-PKD B** | eGFR 25-60 | ACEi+ARB vs ACEi alone → no benefit of combination; combination had more adverse events |

**Exam pearl:** HALT-PKD showed aggressive BP control slows kidney GROWTH but does NOT improve GFR decline in early disease. Dual RAAS blockade has NO benefit in ADPKD.

### Tolvaptan

- Selective vasopressin V2 receptor antagonist
- FDA approved (Jynarque) for adults at risk of rapidly progressing ADPKD
- Mechanism: blocks vasopressin-mediated cAMP production in collecting duct → slows cyst growth
- Eligibility: confirmed ADPKD diagnosis + rapidly progressing disease (Mayo 1C-1E)

### TEMPO 3:4 Trial (Landmark)

- N=1,445, eGFR > 60, TKV >= 750 mL
- Tolvaptan vs placebo for 3 years
- Tolvaptan reduced TKV growth rate by 49% (2.8% vs 5.5%/year)
- Tolvaptan slowed eGFR decline by 26% (-2.72 vs -3.70 mL/min/year)
- Side effects: aquaresis (polyuria, polydipsia, nocturia), hepatotoxicity (monitor LFTs monthly x18 months then q3 months)

### REPRISE Trial

- Extended eligibility to patients with eGFR 25-65 and older patients
- Confirmed tolvaptan slows GFR decline in later-stage ADPKD

### Other Medications

- Statins: may slow kidney volume increase (limited evidence)
- Desmopressin: for cyst bleeding in patients with poor renal function (GFR < 15)
- Antibiotics: for cyst infection (use lipophilic antibiotics that penetrate cysts -- fluoroquinolones, TMP-SMX, chloramphenicol)
- Analgesics: for pain (avoid NSAIDs in CKD)
- Somatostatin analogs: may decrease liver cyst volume

### Lifestyle

- Limit sodium to < 2 g/day
- High water intake (2-3 L/day) for CKD stages 1-3 → suppress vasopressin [SWGIKD Grade D]
  - Cochrane 2024: prescribed water intake did NOT slow eGFR decline and increased hyponatremia risk (RR 4.19)
- Avoid contact sports or activities with abdominal trauma risk (large kidneys)
- Weight management, cardiovascular exercise

## Extrarenal Manifestations (HIGH-YIELD)

| Manifestation | Prevalence | Key Points |
|---|---|---|
| **Hepatic cysts** (polycystic liver) | > 90% of adults > 35 | Most common extrarenal. Usually asymptomatic. Avoid estrogen (OCP, HRT) |
| **Intracranial aneurysm** | 9-12% (22% if FH positive) | Screen with MRA WITHOUT gadolinium. Rupture risk = general population |
| Seminal vesicle cysts | 40% of males | Rarely symptomatic |
| Pancreatic cysts | 10% | Asymptomatic |
| Mitral valve prolapse | ~25% | Usually benign |
| Diverticulosis | Common | |
| Abdominal hernia | Common | |

### Intracranial Aneurysm Screening

- KDIGO does NOT recommend widespread screening
- Screen with MRA (no gadolinium) if:
  - Family history of ICA or subarachnoid hemorrhage
  - Symptoms suggestive (severe headache, loss of consciousness)
  - High-risk occupation (pilot)
  - Prior ICA rupture
  - Preparation for major elective surgery
  - Patient anxiety despite education
- If negative → rescreen every 5-10 years
- If positive → smoking cessation, BP control; neurosurgical evaluation for size/location

## Differential Diagnosis of Bilateral Renal Cysts

| Condition | Key Distinguishing Features |
|---|---|
| ADPKD | Enlarged kidneys, bilateral, hepatic cysts, family history |
| ARPKD | Children, large hyperechogenic kidneys, ESRD in childhood, hepatic fibrosis |
| ADTKD (tubulointerstitial) | Small kidneys, NO enlargement, bland sediment, gout, interstitial fibrosis |
| von Hippel-Lindau | Bilateral cysts + hemangioblastoma + pheochromocytoma + RCC |
| Tuberous sclerosis | Bilateral cysts + angiomyolipomas, skin lesions (angiofibromas, shagreen patch), intact kidney function |
| Acquired cystic disease | Small kidneys, long-standing CKD/dialysis, no family history, ↑ RCC risk |
| Medullary sponge kidney | Medullary nephrocalcinosis, stones, "brush" on IVP |
| Simple cysts | Increase with age, no kidney enlargement, no family history |

## High-Yield Exam Scenarios

1. **35-year-old with family history of ADPKD, ultrasound shows 2 cysts in each kidney**: Meets criteria (>= 3 bilateral at age 30-39). Diagnosis confirmed. No genetic testing needed.

2. **28-year-old ADPKD with eGFR 95, BP 140/90**: Young patient with preserved GFR. Target BP < 110/75 (HALT-PKD A). Start ACEi. Consider tolvaptan if htTKV shows Mayo 1C-1E.

3. **ADPKD patient with acute flank pain, fever, elevated WBC**: Cyst infection. CT with contrast to localize. Blood/urine cultures. Treat with lipophilic antibiotics (fluoroquinolones penetrate cysts best). Urine culture may be NEGATIVE if cyst not communicating with collecting system.

4. **ADPKD patient asks about screening children**: Screening in asymptomatic children with family history is controversial and generally NOT suggested. Monitor blood pressure. Discuss with genetics.

5. **45-year-old ADPKD, htTKV 1200 mL (Mayo 1C), eGFR 70**: Rapidly progressing. Start tolvaptan. Counsel about polyuria, aquaresis (drink freely), hepatotoxicity monitoring (LFTs monthly x18 months). Water restriction is DANGEROUS on tolvaptan.

6. **ADPKD with sudden severe headache**: Rule out ruptured intracranial aneurysm (prevalence 9-12%). Urgent CT head, then MRA or CTA. ICA prevalence is 22% if positive family history of ICA.
