# Genomic Testing in Pediatric and Adult Nephrology

## Exam Logic
*   **Core Concept:** Genomic testing is shifting from a "last resort" (after biopsy/exhaustive workup) to a **first-line diagnostic tool**.
*   **Why it's the correct answer:** In pediatric nephrology, the **diagnostic yield (DY)** is significantly higher than in adults. Early testing is **cost-effective** because it terminates the "diagnostic odyssey," avoids unnecessary invasive biopsies (up to 35% reduction), and informs transplant donor selection.
*   **Common Distractors:** 
    *   *Waiting for biopsy:* Exams may suggest biopsy first; however, genomic testing can now preclude the need for biopsy in specific monogenic cases.
    *   *Family history requirement:* While a positive family history ↑ DY, its absence does **not** rule out a genetic cause (de novo mutations).
*   **Conceptual Pitfalls:** Confusing **Genetics** (single gene/inheritance) with **Genomics** (whole genome/interactions/non-coding regions).

## Clinical Summary

### 1. Definitions
*   **Genetics:** Study of single genes and inheritance patterns.
*   **Genomics:** Study of the entire genome, including interactions between genes and environment (coding and non-coding regions).

### 2. Diagnostic Yield (DY)
DY varies based on population and strategy:
*   **General Adult CKD:** Low (~9.3%).
*   **Suspected Monogenic/Pediatric:** High (30% → 47%).
*   **High-Yield Subtypes:** Glomerular, tubular, and cystic kidney diseases.
*   **Factors that ↑ DY:**
    *   Childhood onset.
    *   Extra-renal manifestations.
    *   Parental consanguinity.
    *   Positive family history.
    *   Use of **Trio testing** (patient + parents).

### 3. Clinical Utility
*   **Therapeutic:** Enables targeted pharmacological therapy and cessation of ineffective meds.
*   **Procedural:** Avoids kidney biopsy in up to 35% of cases.
*   **Prognostic:** Better estimation of disease progression.
*   **Family Health:** Enables **cascade screening** (testing at-risk relatives) and prevents unsuitable living kidney donation.

### 4. Implementation Strategy
*   **Who to test:** Prioritize patients with childhood onset, extra-renal features, or suspected monogenic subtypes.
*   **When to test:** **At point of referral** (initial workup). This is the most cost-effective window to avoid unnecessary downstream investigations.
*   **Testing Modalities:** 
    *   **Exome Sequencing (ES):** Analyzes coding regions.
    *   **Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS):** Analyzes coding and non-coding regions.
    *   **Chromosomal Microarray (CMA):** Detects copy number variants (CNVs).
