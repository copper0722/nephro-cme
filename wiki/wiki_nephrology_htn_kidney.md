# Hypertension-Induced Kidney Disease -- Nephrosclerosis

## Exam Logic Summary

HTN-induced kidney disease questions test: (1) clinical diagnosis of exclusion (biopsy rarely performed), (2) distinguishing benign vs malignant nephrosclerosis, (3) APOL1-associated FSGS in African Americans (HTN is consequence, not cause), (4) BP targets and ACEi/ARB as first-line, (5) AASK trial results (intensive BP did NOT reduce renal endpoints in African Americans), (6) pathology findings (arteriolar hyalinosis, global glomerulosclerosis). Key distractor: assuming hypertension always CAUSES CKD -- in many African American patients, APOL1 variants cause the kidney disease and HTN is secondary.

**Textbook ref:** Brenner Ch. 40 (Hypertensive Kidney Disease), Harrison Ch. 278 (Vascular Diseases of the Kidney)

---

## Definition

- Hypertensive nephrosclerosis: pathological findings on biopsy including arteriosclerosis, arteriole thickening, afferent arteriole hyalinization, secondary chronic glomerulopathy, tubulointerstitial scarring
- Clinical definition: chronic essential HTN + hypertensive retinopathy + LVH + mild proteinuria (typically <= 1 g/day) + progressive kidney dysfunction
- Also called: hypertensive nephropathy, arterionephrosclerosis, arteriolonephrosclerosis
- Historically considered 2nd leading cause of ESKD; prevalence may be OVERESTIMATED (diagnosis of exclusion without biopsy confirmation)
- More common in Black people (especially African American ancestry) and older adults

---

## Epidemiology

- HTN present in 67%-92% of CKD patients; prevalence increases as kidney function worsens
- Biopsy-proven prevalence: < 1%-8% in older adults; 6%-27% in ESKD
- China (2010-2015): 28.5% of CKD attributed to HTN
- Norway (biopsy registry): 18.6% prevalence of biopsy-proven hypertensive nephrosclerosis

## Risk Factors

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Blood pressure** | >= 140/90: HR 1.97 for minor nephropathy; HR 8.12 for major nephropathy |
| **APOL1 gene** | 2 risk alleles (G1/G2) in African Americans → FSGS/ESKD independent of HTN |
| **UMOD gene** | Affects sodium-potassium-chloride cotransporter → BP regulation |
| **THP gene** | Increases renal susceptibility to HTN damage in African descent |
| **CHGA gene** | Polymorphisms → nephrosclerosis in men of African descent |
| **Low nephron number** | Prematurity, impaired intrauterine growth |
| **Ethnicity** | African American ancestry (highest risk) |
| **Age** | Older age (aOR 1.03 per year) |
| **Male sex** | aOR 1.3 |
| **Lifestyle** | Western diet, smoking |

## Pathogenesis

### Hemodynamic Mechanism
1. Progressive decline in autoregulation → abnormal afferent arteriole dilation → elevated intraglomerular pressure
2. Direct transmission of systemic hypertension → pulsation, stretching, endothelial injury
3. Oxidative stress, RAAS activation, NF-kB transcription, TGF-beta synthesis
4. Arteriolar adaptation: smooth muscle → myofibroblasts → wall stiffness (arteriolosclerosis)
5. Media hyalinosis of afferent arteriole → decreased filtration
6. Glomerular: collagen replaces tuft → global glomerulosclerosis
7. Tubulointerstitial: cell atrophy → proteinuria; epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition → fibrosis

### Benign vs Malignant Nephrosclerosis

| Feature | Benign (55%) | Malignant (45%) |
|---|---|---|
| Age | Older (mean 47 years) | Younger (mean 39 years) |
| HTN duration | Longer (6 years) | Shorter (3 years) |
| BP | 182/119 mmHg | 213/138 mmHg |
| Retinopathy grade III/IV | 10.7% | 48.6% |
| Mean eGFR | 60.5 mL/min/1.73 m² | 34.5 mL/min/1.73 m² |
| Pathology | Arteriolar hyalinosis, intimal fibrosis, medial hypertrophy | Fibrinoid necrosis, myointimal proliferation → acute TMA |

**Exam pearl:** Malignant nephrosclerosis presents as acute TMA (thrombotic microangiopathy) on biopsy -- fibrinoid necrosis of glomeruli, arterioles, and interlobular arteries. This is the same histological pattern as other causes of TMA (HUS/TTP). Clinical context (severely elevated BP + retinopathy + renal failure) distinguishes it.

## APOL1 and Hypertensive Kidney Disease (CRITICAL CONCEPT)

- APOL1 gene variants (G1, G2) cause glomerulosclerosis independent of hypertension in African Americans
- In APOL1-associated kidney disease: HTN is a CONSEQUENCE of CKD, not the cause
- Standard ACEi/ARB often INEFFECTIVE at protecting against CKD progression in APOL1-mediated disease
- APOL1-associated nephropathy may be misdiagnosed as "hypertensive nephrosclerosis" because biopsy is rarely performed

**Exam pearl:** The exam may present an African American patient with "hypertensive nephrosclerosis" who progresses despite optimal BP control with ACEi. The key concept: this may be APOL1-associated FSGS masquerading as hypertensive kidney disease. APOL1 genotyping should be considered. This distinction matters because the pathophysiology and prognosis differ.

## Clinical Presentation

- Typically: severe or chronic poorly controlled HTN
- Hypertensive retinopathy, LVH
- Progressive renal dysfunction (slowly declining GFR)
- Absent or low-grade proteinuria (< 1 g/day or ACR < 500 mcg/mg)
- Bland urinalysis (no active sediment in benign form)
- Clinical criteria: sensitivity 17%, specificity 94%

### Clinical Diagnostic Criteria

**Schlessinger (non-African descent):**
- FH of HTN in first-degree relatives
- LVH
- Proteinuria < 0.5 g/day or <= 1++ on dipstick
- Chronic HTN (BP > 140/90) PRIOR to onset of proteinuria/SCr > 1.2
- No nephrotoxic agent use
- No clinically relevant kidney disease history

**AASK (African descent):**
- Age 18-70
- DBP > 95 mmHg
- UPCR < 2 g/g
- No immunological kidney disease or diabetes

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Distinguishing Feature |
|---|---|
| **Normal aging** | Less severe changes; glomerular involution; afferent arteriole hyalinosis |
| **FSGS** | Podocytopathy; collapsing GN; tubulointerstitial inflammation out of proportion to glomerular lesions |
| **Diabetic kidney disease** | Mesangial expansion, GBM thickening, nodular glomerulosclerosis (Kimmelstiel-Wilson) |
| **IgA nephropathy** | Mesangial IgA deposits on IF |
| **Membranous nephropathy** | Subepithelial deposits, GBM thickening |
| **Alport syndrome** | GBM lamellation on EM |
| **AERD (cholesterol emboli)** | Cholesterol clefts in arterioles; history of vascular procedure |

## Biopsy Findings

### Benign Hypertensive Nephrosclerosis
- Arteriolar nephrosclerosis (focal global glomerulosclerosis)
- Afferent arteriole hyalinosis (hyaline deposits in vessel wall)
- Intimal fibrosis
- Pericapsular fibrosis
- Tubulointerstitial atrophy and fibrosis
- IF: negative

### Malignant Nephrosclerosis
- Fibrinoid necrosis of arteriolar/arterial walls (acute lesion)
- Subendothelial widening ("onion-skin" intimal thickening)
- Thrombi visible on light microscopy
- Mesangiolysis, hypertrophic glomerulosclerosis
- Acute TMA pattern

**Exam pearl:** Afferent arteriole hyalinosis = benign hypertensive nephrosclerosis. "Onion-skin" intimal thickening + fibrinoid necrosis = malignant nephrosclerosis. Both can occur in the SAME kidney if malignant HTN develops on background of chronic HTN.

## Management

### Blood Pressure Targets

| Population | Target | Source |
|---|---|---|
| CKD not on dialysis | SBP < 120 mmHg (if tolerated) | KDIGO 2024 (Level 2, Grade B) |
| CKD with proteinuria | < 130/80 mmHg | ACC/AHA (Class I) |
| CKD transplant | < 130/80 mmHg | KDIGO |
| CKD on dialysis | Individualized | No clear target; avoid intradialytic hypotension |

### Pharmacotherapy
- **ACEi/ARB = first-line** for CKD with hypertension [Strong]
- ACEi reduce major CVD events in CKD (DynaMed Level 1); may reduce ESRD in macroalbuminuric CKD (Level 2)
- ARBs may reduce ESRD in CKD with macroalbuminuria (Level 2)
- CCBs: may reduce CVD events; inconsistent renal protection data
- Diuretics/beta-blockers: similar CVD event reduction as ACEi/CCB
- Do NOT combine ACEi + ARB (ONTARGET: hyperkalemia + worsening renal function without benefit)
- Stopping ACEi: K+ > 5.6 mmol/L or SCr rise > 30% from baseline

### Lifestyle
- Sodium < 2 g/day (DASH diet)
- Weight loss, exercise, smoking cessation, limit alcohol

### Key Trial: AASK (African American Study of Kidney Disease)

- N = 1,094; African Americans with hypertensive nephropathy
- Intensive MAP <= 92 mmHg vs standard MAP 102-107 mmHg
- **Result: intensive BP control did NOT reduce composite renal endpoint** (GFR decline, ESRD, death)
- Ramipril (ACEi) superior to amlodipine (CCB) for renal protection in proteinuric subgroup
- Bottom line: in African Americans with presumed hypertensive nephrosclerosis, ACEi is preferred agent; intensive BP lowering beyond standard targets not clearly beneficial

**Exam pearl:** AASK is THE trial for hypertensive kidney disease in African Americans. Two key findings: (1) intensive BP target did NOT help, (2) ACEi (ramipril) was superior to CCB (amlodipine) for renal protection. If the exam asks about antihypertensive choice in an African American with hypertensive nephropathy, the answer is ACEi.

## High-Yield Exam Scenarios

1. **55-year-old African American man with HTN for 20 years, LVH, SCr 2.0, proteinuria 0.4 g/day, bland urinalysis. Most likely diagnosis?** Hypertensive nephrosclerosis (clinical diagnosis of exclusion). Consider APOL1 testing if available. Start ACEi if not already on one.

2. **40-year-old with sudden severe HTN (230/140), headache, blurred vision, SCr rising from 1.0 to 3.5 in 1 week, grade IV retinopathy. Biopsy shows fibrinoid necrosis and "onion-skin" intimal thickening.** Malignant nephrosclerosis (hypertensive emergency with acute TMA). Treat with IV antihypertensives (nitroprusside, nicardipine). Gradual BP reduction (no more than 25% in first few hours).

3. **African American patient with hypertensive nephropathy on maximal ACEi, BP well controlled at 125/78, eGFR continues to decline. What to consider?** APOL1-associated kidney disease -- may explain progression despite optimal BP control. ACEi may not be protective in APOL1-mediated disease. Genetic testing if available.

4. **Patient with CKD stage 3 from HTN. Which antihypertensive agent is preferred?** ACEi or ARB (AASK trial: ACEi superior to CCB for renal protection). Target SBP < 120 mmHg if tolerated (KDIGO 2024).
