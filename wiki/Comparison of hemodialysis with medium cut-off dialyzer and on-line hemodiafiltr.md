# Wiki: Medium Cut-Off (MCO) Dialyzers vs. On-line HDF

## Overview
Comparison of solute clearance between **Medium Cut-Off (MCO)** dialyzers (e.g., Theranova) used in standard Hemodialysis (HD) and **On-line Hemodiafiltration (ol-HDF)** using high-flux membranes.

## Key Concepts & Definitions
*   **Small Molecules (< 500 Da):** e.g., Urea, Creatinine. Removed via diffusion.
*   **Middle Molecules (> 500 Da):** e.g., $\beta_2$-microglobulin (11.8 kDa), Myoglobin (17 kDa). Removal depends on membrane permeability (pore size) and convective transport.
*   **MCO Dialyzer:** Novel membranes designed to increase the removal of molecules $> 25$ kDa while attempting to minimize albumin loss.
*   **ol-HDF:** Combines diffusion and extensive convection (substitution volumes) to enhance middle-molecule clearance.

## Comparative Efficacy (Exam Logic)
Based on the study (Belmouaz et al.), there is **no significant difference** in the removal of the following between MCO-HD and ol-HDF:

| Solute | Result | Clinical Significance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Urea / Creatinine** | Equivalent | Small molecule clearance is not modality-dependent in these setups. |
| **$\beta_2$-microglobulin** | Equivalent | RR $\approx 81\%$ for both; MCO pores provide diffusion-based clearance similar to ol-HDF convection. |
| **Myoglobin** | Equivalent | RR $\approx 60-61\%$ for both. |
| **Nutritional Markers** | Equivalent | No significant change in serum albumin or prealbumin. |
| **Hemodynamic Stability** | Equivalent | No significant difference in intradialytic hypotensive episodes. |

## Exam High-Yield Points
*   **Mechanism Shift:** MCO-HD achieves "HDF-like" clearance of middle molecules through **increased membrane permeability (diffusion)** rather than **convective fluid exchange**.
*   **Clinical Utility:** MCO dialyzers may offer a simplified alternative to ol-HDF (no need for large substitution volumes or complex volume-control systems) while maintaining similar clearance of $\beta_2$-m and myoglobin.
*   **Safety Profile:** Albumin loss is a theoretical concern with MCO; however, this data suggests nutritional stability (albumin/prealbumin) is maintained.
