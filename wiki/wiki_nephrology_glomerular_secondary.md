# Secondary Glomerular Diseases, RPGN, and Hereditary Nephropathy

## Exam Logic Summary

This file covers diseases presenting primarily as nephritic syndrome, RPGN, or hereditary glomerulopathy. The exam tests: (1) RPGN classification by IF pattern (linear vs granular vs pauci-immune), (2) ANCA specificity (PR3 vs MPO and their disease associations), (3) anti-GBM disease management urgency, (4) Alport syndrome genetics and pathognomonic findings. Critical exam skill: rapid triage of RPGN by serology (anti-GBM, ANCA, ANA, complement) before biopsy returns.

**Textbook ref:** Brenner Ch. 31 (RPGN), Ch. 32 (Vasculitis), Ch. 33 (Anti-GBM), Ch. 47 (Alport); KDIGO 2021 Glomerular Diseases Guideline

## Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN)

### Definition and Key Facts

- Clinical syndrome of rapid GFR decline (weeks to months) with nephritic features
- Histologically = crescentic GN: crescents in > 50% of glomeruli
- Crescents = proliferative lesions involving >= 10% of Bowman capsule circumference
- Classification based on IF pattern is the MOST important framework

### RPGN Classification (Exam Critical)

| Type | IF Pattern | Mechanism | Frequency | Key Disease |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Type 1 (Linear)** | Linear IgG along GBM | Anti-GBM antibodies | ~10% | Anti-GBM disease (Goodpasture) |
| **Type 2 (Granular)** | Granular Ig + complement | Immune complex deposition | 15-20% | Lupus nephritis, IgAN, post-infectious, MPGN, HSP |
| **Type 3 (Pauci-immune)** | Absent/faint Ig | ANCA-mediated | **60-80%** (most common) | GPA, MPA, EGPA, idiopathic |

**Exam pearl:** Type 3 (pauci-immune) is the MOST COMMON type of crescentic GN. 27-38% of pauci-immune type are ANCA-negative (idiopathic). Negative ANCA does NOT rule out pauci-immune crescentic GN.

**Exam pearl:** "Double positive" = anti-GBM + ANCA (usually MPO). Occurs in ~27% of anti-GBM patients. Renal prognosis follows anti-GBM pattern (poor) but may have relapses like ANCA disease.

### RPGN Emergency Workup

Immediate labs when RPGN suspected:
1. Serum creatinine + eGFR
2. Anti-GBM antibodies (ELISA)
3. ANCA (PR3 and MPO by immunoassay)
4. ANA + anti-dsDNA + complement (C3, C4)
5. Urinalysis with sediment (dysmorphic RBCs, RBC casts)
6. Cryoglobulins (if low complement)
7. **Renal biopsy ASAP** [Strong Recommendation per KDIGO]

**Exam pearl:** Do NOT wait for serologic results to initiate treatment if clinical suspicion is high. Start empiric pulse steroids + plasmapheresis while awaiting confirmation.

### Complement Levels in RPGN Differential

| Disease | C3 | C4 | Key Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anti-GBM | Normal | Normal | Linear IgG on IF |
| ANCA vasculitis | Normal | Normal | Pauci-immune on IF |
| IgAN | Normal | Normal | Mesangial IgA |
| Lupus nephritis | **Low** | **Low** | "Full house" IF |
| MPGN | **Low** | Low or normal | Subendothelial deposits |
| Post-infectious | **Low** | Normal or low | "Starry sky" C3, subepithelial humps |
| Cryoglobulinemia | Normal or low | **Low** | Cryoglobulins, HCV |

### Biopsy Findings by RPGN Type

| Type | LM | IF | EM |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Type 1 (Linear)** | Crescents + focal necrotizing GN; NO intracapillary proliferation | **Linear IgG** along GBM | No electron-dense deposits |
| **Type 2 (Granular)** | Crescents + intracapillary proliferation + fibrinoid necrosis (in lupus) | **Granular** Ig + complement | Subendothelial/subepithelial deposits depending on cause |
| **Type 3 (Pauci-immune)** | Crescents + focal necrotizing GN + fibrinoid necrosis; granuloma (GPA) | **Absent or faint** Ig/complement | No deposits |

### Crescent Staging

| Stage | Composition | Reversibility |
|---|---|---|
| Cellular | > 75% cells/fibrin, < 25% fibrous | Potentially reversible |
| Fibrocellular | 25-75% cells, 25-75% fibrous | Partially reversible |
| Fibrous | < 25% cells, > 75% fibrous | Irreversible |

**Exam pearl:** Fibrous crescents = chronic damage = unlikely to respond to immunosuppression. Cellular crescents = active disease = treat aggressively.

### Treatment Overview by Type

| Type | Induction | Maintenance |
|---|---|---|
| **Type 1** | Pulse steroids + CYC + plasmapheresis | None (relapse rare) |
| **Type 2** | Treat underlying disease (steroids + CYC for lupus; supportive for post-infectious) | Disease-specific |
| **Type 3** | Steroids + CYC or rituximab | Rituximab (preferred) or azathioprine |

### Prognosis

- 5-year cumulative renal survival: linear 17.6%, granular 70.1%, pauci-immune 44.3%
- Poor prognostic factors: older age, high creatinine at presentation, oligoanuria, > 50% fibrous crescents, dialysis dependence at presentation
- Linear (type 1) has worst renal prognosis but may have better overall survival if treated early

## Anti-GBM Disease (Goodpasture Disease)

### Key Facts

- Rare autoimmune disease: 0.5-1 per million/year
- Antibodies target alpha-3 chain of type IV collagen (NC1 domain) in GBM and alveolar basement membrane
- Bimodal age distribution: peak at 20-30 (males, pulmonary + renal) and 60-70 (females, renal-limited)
- Clinical spectrum: 60-80% renal + pulmonary; 20-40% renal only; < 10% pulmonary only
- Up to 20% of RPGN/crescentic GN is anti-GBM mediated
- ANCA co-positivity in ~27% (usually MPO)

### Risk Factors

- **Pulmonary injury triggers**: smoking, cocaine, hydrocarbon solvents, hard metals, respiratory infections (influenza), alemtuzumab
- **Genetic**: HLA-DR15 (HLA-DR2), HLA-DRB1*03, HLA-DRB1*04
- **Alport syndrome post-transplant**: transplanted kidney has "normal" alpha-3/4/5 collagen IV that Alport patients have never been exposed to -> de novo anti-GBM disease (3-4% incidence, 75% graft failure)

### Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Dyspnea | 79% |
| Hemoptysis | 75% |
| Fatigue | 64% |
| Cough | 64% |
| Hematuria | 36% (macroscopic) |
| Edema | 32% |
| Fever | 43% |

**Exam pearl:** Smokers get pulmonary + renal disease; non-smokers often get renal-limited disease. Pulmonary hemorrhage WITHOUT renal involvement is rare (< 10%).

### Diagnosis

- **Serology**: anti-GBM antibodies by ELISA and/or Western blot (sensitivity 93%, specificity 97%)
  - Chemiluminescence immunoassay: sensitivity 95.6%, specificity 99.6%
- **Biopsy**: linear IgG along GBM + crescentic GN
- CXR: bilateral air-space consolidation (perihilar, sparing apices) -- may be normal despite pulmonary hemorrhage
- HRCT to confirm diffuse alveolar hemorrhage
- **Always check ANCA** (27% double-positive; treatment implications)

**Exam pearl:** Negative anti-GBM ELISA does not fully exclude disease. IgG4-restricted anti-GBM may be missed by standard ELISA. If clinical suspicion high, proceed to biopsy.

### Treatment (KDIGO 2021 -- Medical Emergency)

**Triple therapy is standard:**

1. **Corticosteroids**: methylprednisolone 500-1,000 mg/day IV x 3 days, then prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (taper to 20 mg/day by 6 weeks, discontinue by 6 months)
2. **Cyclophosphamide**: 2-3 mg/kg/day orally x 2-3 months (reduce to 2 mg/kg in patients > 55)
3. **Plasmapheresis**: daily exchange with 4.5-5% albumin, 50 mL/kg (max 4 L) x 14 days or until anti-GBM undetectable
   - Use FFP instead of albumin after kidney biopsy or with active hemorrhage

**Key treatment principles:**
- START treatment immediately if diagnosis suspected (even before biopsy/serology confirmation)
- If pulmonary hemorrhage: begin immunosuppressive therapy regardless of kidney prognosis [Strong Recommendation]
- Patients dialysis-dependent at presentation + > 85% crescents on biopsy: unlikely to recover kidney function; treat with dialysis alone UNLESS pulmonary hemorrhage present

**Poor prognostic indicators (kidney recovery unlikely):**
- Serum creatinine > 4 mg/dL (> 500 mcmol/L)
- Oligoanuria
- > 50% crescents on biopsy
- Dialysis-dependent at presentation + 85-100% glomerulosclerosis

**Post-treatment:**
- No maintenance therapy needed (relapse rare, <= 6%)
- Defer kidney transplant until anti-GBM antibodies undetectable for >= 6 months
- Alport post-transplant anti-GBM: uncommon but severe (3-4%); more common with large gene deletions

## GPA and MPA (ANCA-Associated Vasculitis)

### Key Facts

- GPA (formerly Wegener) and MPA are the most common ANCA-associated vasculitides (AAV)
- Small-vessel vasculitis with few or no immune deposits (pauci-immune)
- Prevalence: 200-400 per million (AAV overall); GPA 2.3-140/million; MPA 2.7-94/million
- Peak onset age 65-74; males = females; more common in White populations
- Risk factors: silica exposure, smoking (MPO-ANCA), infections (S. aureus for GPA), drugs (anti-thyroid, hydralazine, cocaine/levamisole)

### GPA vs MPA (Exam Critical)

| Feature | GPA | MPA |
|---|---|---|
| ANCA pattern | c-ANCA/PR3 (80-90%) | p-ANCA/MPO (60%) |
| Upper airway | YES (70-100%): sinusitis, saddle nose, subglottic stenosis | NO |
| Lung | Nodules, cavities, hemorrhage (30%) | Capillaritis, DAH, ILD |
| Kidney | ~70% (less severe) | > 90% (more severe) |
| Granulomas | YES (necrotizing granulomatous inflammation) | NO |
| Cardiovascular | Up to 44% | Rare |
| Ocular | 50% (scleritis, proptosis) | 50% |
| Neurologic | 25% (CNS common) | 22-37% (peripheral > CNS) |
| Ear/nose/throat | 70-100% | Not typically |

**Exam pearl:** GPA = granulomas + upper airway. MPA = NO granulomas, NO upper airway. Both cause pauci-immune crescentic GN. If biopsy shows granuloma = GPA. If no granuloma + pauci-immune GN = could be either (use ANCA to differentiate).

**Exam pearl:** PR3-ANCA (c-ANCA) = GPA. MPO-ANCA (p-ANCA) = MPA (and EGPA). ANCA is negative in up to 20% of GPA (especially limited upper airway disease) and 10% of MPA. Negative ANCA does NOT exclude diagnosis.

### ANCA Testing

- Use antigen-specific immunoassay (PR3 and MPO ELISA) as PRIMARY screening method [Strong Recommendation per 2017 International Consensus]
- c-ANCA: diffuse cytoplasmic granular fluorescence (= PR3 target)
- p-ANCA: perinuclear staining (= MPO target)
- a-ANCA: atypical pattern -- drug-induced, IBD, RA (typically without vasculitis)

### ACR/EULAR 2022 Classification Criteria

**GPA** (score >= 5 points):
- Nasal symptoms (ulcers, crusting, bloody discharge, septal abnormalities) +3
- Cartilaginous involvement (ear/nose cartilage inflammation, hoarse voice, saddle nose, endobronchial) +2
- Hearing loss +1
- c-ANCA or anti-PR3 +5
- Pulmonary nodules/mass/cavitation on imaging +2
- Granuloma/giant cells on biopsy +2
- Nasal/paranasal sinus inflammation on imaging +1
- Pauci-immune GN on biopsy +1
- p-ANCA or anti-MPO -1
- Blood eosinophils >= 1 x 10^9/L -4

**MPA** (score >= 5 points):
- Nasal involvement (ulcers, crusting, congestion, blockage, septal defect) -3
- p-ANCA or anti-MPO +6
- Chest imaging: fibrosis or ILD +3
- Pauci-immune GN on biopsy +3
- c-ANCA or anti-PR3 -1
- Blood eosinophils >= 1 x 10^9/L -4

### Renal Involvement

- Kidney affected in ~70% GPA and > 90% MPA
- Often asymptomatic initially (detected on urinalysis screening)
- Manifestations: hematuria, proteinuria, HTN, rising creatinine, pauci-immune crescentic GN
- Biopsy: necrotizing crescentic GN with absent/minimal immune deposits

### Treatment (KDIGO 2021 + EULAR/ERA-EDTA 2024)

**Induction (organ-threatening or life-threatening):**
- Glucocorticoids + rituximab OR cyclophosphamide [Strong Recommendation]
  - Rituximab typically preferred, especially in GPA (RAVE, RITUXVAS trials)
  - CYC preferred if severe renal involvement (creatinine > 300-500 mcmol/L)
- Adjunctive plasmapheresis: consider if serum creatinine > 300-500 mcmol/L (3.4-5.6 mg/dL) [Conditional Recommendation]
- IVIG for refractory GPA [Conditional Recommendation]
- Avacopan (complement C5a receptor inhibitor): can be added to reduce glucocorticoid exposure [Conditional Recommendation]

**Induction dosing:**
- Rituximab: 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 doses OR 1 g x 2 (day 1 and day 15)
- CYC: 2 mg/kg/day oral (max 200 mg/day) x 3-6 months; reduce dose in elderly (> 60) and renal impairment
- Methylprednisolone: 500-1,000 mg/day IV x 3 days, then oral prednisone taper

**Maintenance (after remission achieved):**
- Rituximab preferred [Conditional Recommendation]: 500 mg IV q6 months (MAINRITSAN) or 1,000 mg IV q4-6 months
- Alternative: azathioprine 2 mg/kg/day (max 200 mg/day) or methotrexate (up to 25 mg/week, if adequate renal function)
- MMF if intolerance to rituximab, azathioprine, and methotrexate [Conditional Recommendation]
- CYC is NOT used for maintenance (toxicity: malignancy, infertility)
- Continue maintenance for 6-12 months after complete remission; withdrawal timing uncertain

**Monitoring:**
- Disease status every 3 months during induction, then q6-12 months
- ANCA levels monthly during acute phase, q1-3 months during maintenance
- Rising ANCA may predict relapse (especially PR3) but is NOT an indication for preemptive treatment escalation alone
- Monitor for drug toxicity: CBC, LFTs, renal function regularly

**Prophylaxis:**
- TMP-SMX for PCP prophylaxis in all patients on immunosuppression [Strong Recommendation]
- TMP-SMX may also reduce GPA relapse rate (nasal S. aureus)

**Exam pearl:** RAVE trial: rituximab non-inferior to CYC for induction of remission in AAV. MAINRITSAN trial: rituximab superior to azathioprine for maintenance. Rituximab has become the primary treatment choice for pauci-immune crescentic GN.

## Alport Syndrome

### Key Facts

- Genetic disorder of type IV collagen (alpha-3, alpha-4, alpha-5 chains) in GBM, cochlea, and lens
- Prevalence: 1:5,000 (gene mutation); 1:10,000 (XLAS); 1:50,000 (ARAS)
- Clinical triad: progressive renal failure + sensorineural hearing loss + ocular abnormalities
- No curative treatment

### Genetics (Exam Critical)

| Type | Gene | Inheritance | Frequency | ESRD Timing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **XLAS** | COL4A5 | X-linked | ~80% | Males: < 30 (juvenile) or > 30 (adult) by mutation type |
| **ARAS** | COL4A3 or COL4A4 (biallelic) | AR | ~15% | < 30 in most |
| **ADAS** | COL4A3 or COL4A4 (single) | AD | ~5% | Later, slower progression |

**Exam pearl:** Single heterozygous COL4A3/COL4A4 mutations can cause EITHER autosomal dominant Alport OR thin basement membrane nephropathy (TBMN). TBMN = carrier state for ARAS; usually benign (persistent hematuria, no progressive renal disease, no extrarenal findings).

### Clinical Presentation by Type

**X-linked males (juvenile form):**
- Persistent microhematuria from birth
- Proteinuria by late childhood/early adolescence
- Hearing loss by late childhood (80-90% by age 40)
- ESRD by age 25-30 (truncating mutations); age 37 (missense mutations)
- Ocular findings in 30-40%

**X-linked females (carriers):**
- > 90% have microscopic hematuria (may be intermittent)
- Most develop proteinuria, HTN eventually
- 12% reach ESRD by age 40
- Hearing loss less frequent, occurs later

**ARAS (both sexes equally severe):**
- Persistent microhematuria
- Significant proteinuria by late childhood
- ESRD typically < 30 years
- Hearing loss in 66%

**ADAS:**
- Mildest form; slower progression
- Renal failure of varying severity
- Hearing loss may be very late
- Ocular involvement uncommon

### Mutation Type and Clinical Severity (XLAS)

| Mutation Type | ESRD Rate | Median ESRD Age | Hearing Loss | Ocular Changes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Large deletion | 66.7% | 22 years | 80.8% | 50% |
| Missense | 54.8% | 37 years | 50.8% | 20.3% |
| Small deletion | 75% | 22 years | 95.4% | 36.4% |
| Splice site | 71.4% | 28 years | 86% | 48.9% |
| Truncating | 59.7% | 25 years | 95.5% | 50% |

### Staging

| Stage | Feature |
|---|---|
| 0 | Microscopic hematuria, no microalbuminuria |
| 1 | Microalbuminuria (30-300 mg/g creatinine) |
| 2 | Gross proteinuria (> 300 mg/g creatinine) |
| 3 | GFR < 60 mL/min/1.73m2 |
| 4 | End-stage renal disease |

### Pathognomonic Findings

**EM (diagnostic gold standard for biopsy):**
- Lamellation ("basket weave") of GBM lamina densa
- Splitting of lamina densa into interlacing strands with electron-dense bodies in lacunae
- Diffusely thickened capillary wall
- Variable GBM thickness (thin + thick segments)

**IF/immunohistochemistry:**
- Absent staining for alpha-3, alpha-4, alpha-5 (IV) collagen chains in ~80% of XLAS males
- Patchy/mosaic loss in XLAS females
- Absent alpha-3 and alpha-4 in GBM but positive in Bowman capsule/tubular BM in ARAS

**Ocular findings (pathognomonic):**
- **Anterior lenticonus**: conical protrusion of anterior lens surface; "oil drop" reflex on slit lamp; 10-40% of XLAS/ARAS; NOT seen in XLAS females
- **Perimacular flecks**: 30-50% of XLAS/ARAS; very high specificity for Alport
- Posterior subcapsular cataract
- Corneal dystrophy

**Hearing loss:**
- NOT congenital (develops later)
- Progressive, bilateral, sensorineural
- Affects medium and high frequencies (2,000-8,000 Hz)
- Detectable by audiometry in late childhood (boys) or later (girls)

**Exam pearl:** Anterior lenticonus is pathognomonic for Alport syndrome. No other glomerular disease causes it. If exam shows a young man with hematuria, hearing loss, and anterior lenticonus = Alport.

### Diagnosis

- **Genetic testing** = gold standard (COL4A3, COL4A4, COL4A5 sequencing)
- **Kidney biopsy**: lamellation on EM + absent alpha chains on immunohistochemistry
- **Skin biopsy**: absent alpha-5 (IV) chain staining on epidermal basement membrane (less invasive alternative for XLAS; 86% sensitivity in males)
- Molecular genetic testing results:
  - COL4A5 mutation = XLAS
  - Biallelic COL4A3 or COL4A4 = ARAS
  - Single COL4A3 or COL4A4 = ADAS

### Differential Diagnosis

| Disease | Key Distinguishing Feature |
|---|---|
| **Thin basement membrane nephropathy** | Uniformly thin GBM (< 250 nm), no lamellation, no hearing loss, no ocular findings; same COL4A3/A4 genes (carrier state for ARAS) |
| **IgA nephropathy** | Mesangial IgA on IF; no extrarenal findings |
| **Fechtner syndrome** | Nephritis + deafness + cataracts + macrothrombocytopenia + leukocyte inclusions (MYH9 mutation) |
| **Nail-patella syndrome** | FSGS + hypoplastic nails/patella + dysplasia (LMX1B mutation) |

### Treatment

**No curative treatment. Goals: delay ESRD, manage complications.**

**RAAS blockade (mainstay):**
- ACE inhibitors considered first-line: ramipril 1-2 mg/m2/day (max 6 mg/m2/day)
- Start when overt proteinuria appears (UPCR > 0.2 mg/mg in children)
- Start even at microalbuminuria stage in high-risk boys (COL4A5 deletions/truncating mutations, family history ESRD < 30)
- ARB as second-line (add to ACEi, or use alone if ACEi intolerant)
- Spironolactone as third-line
- Goal: UPCR < 0.5 mg/mg AND >= 50% reduction from baseline

**Key evidence:**
- RAAS inhibitors decrease risk of ESRD (HR 0.33, 95% CI 0.24-0.45) in systematic review of 1,182 patients
- ACEi delayed median dialysis onset from age 22 (untreated) to 40 (treated at proteinuria stage) in Kidney Int 2012 cohort
- EARLY PRO-TECT Alport trial: ramipril slowed disease progression in children with stage 0-1

**SGLT2 inhibitors:**
- Emerging data: albuminuria decreased after starting dapagliflozin in cohort of 112 Alport patients (Kidney Int Rep 2024)
- eGFR initially dipped then stabilized (expected hemodynamic effect)
- Longer-term data needed

**Kidney transplant:**
- Excellent option; similar post-transplant prognosis to other causes of ESRD
- **De novo anti-GBM disease post-transplant**: 3-4% risk (more common with large COL4A5 deletions)
  - Recipient exposed to "normal" alpha-3/4/5 collagen for first time
  - 75% result in irreversible graft failure
  - 75% of cases occur within first year post-transplant
- **Donor selection for XLAS**: optimal living related donor = male with normal urinalysis (unaffected). Female carriers can donate if >= 40, normal function, no proteinuria, no hearing loss.

## High-Yield Comparison: RPGN Causes

### Serology-Based Triage (Exam Algorithm)

```
RPGN suspected (rising Cr + active sediment)
  |
  +--> Check: anti-GBM, ANCA (PR3/MPO), ANA/dsDNA, C3/C4
  |
  +--> Anti-GBM (+) --> Type 1 crescentic GN
  |     Also check ANCA (27% double-positive)
  |     Treatment: pulse steroids + CYC + PLEX (EMERGENCY)
  |
  +--> ANCA (+), anti-GBM (-) --> Type 3 pauci-immune
  |     PR3 --> GPA (check ENT, lungs, eyes)
  |     MPO --> MPA (check lungs, kidneys)
  |     Treatment: steroids + rituximab or CYC
  |
  +--> Low complement, granular Ig on biopsy --> Type 2 immune complex
  |     Low C3+C4 --> lupus
  |     Low C3, normal C4 --> post-infectious, C3 glomerulopathy
  |     Low C4, normal/low C3 --> cryoglobulinemia
  |
  +--> All negative --> ANCA-negative pauci-immune (27-38% of type 3)
        Treat as type 3
```

### Treatment Urgency Comparison

| Disease | Urgency | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Anti-GBM** | EXTREME (hours) | Irreversible if crescents become fibrous; pulmonary hemorrhage can be fatal |
| **ANCA vasculitis** | HIGH (days) | Progressive but more recoverable than anti-GBM |
| **Lupus RPGN** | HIGH (days) | Reversible with aggressive treatment |
| **IgAN crescentic** | MODERATE (days-weeks) | Less aggressive than anti-GBM/ANCA |
| **Post-infectious** | LOW (supportive) | Usually self-limited |

## High-Yield Exam Scenarios

1. **Young man with hemoptysis + rapidly rising creatinine + hematuria**: Anti-GBM disease. Emergency. Start pulse steroids + CYC + plasmapheresis IMMEDIATELY. Check anti-GBM and ANCA (27% double-positive). Biopsy will show linear IgG + crescents.

2. **Middle-aged woman with sinusitis, saddle-nose deformity, pulmonary nodules, hematuria**: GPA. Check c-ANCA/PR3. Biopsy (renal or nasal): necrotizing granulomatous vasculitis. Treatment: rituximab or CYC + steroids.

3. **Elderly patient with AKI, pauci-immune crescentic GN, p-ANCA/MPO positive**: MPA. Treatment: steroids + rituximab (preferred in elderly to reduce CYC toxicity). Add PLEX if Cr > 500 mcmol/L. Maintenance: rituximab q6 months.

4. **Teen boy with persistent hematuria, bilateral sensorineural hearing loss, anterior lenticonus on slit lamp**: Alport syndrome (XLAS). Confirm with genetic testing (COL4A5). Start ACEi immediately. Refer to ophthalmology, audiology, genetics.

5. **Anti-GBM disease, dialysis-dependent at presentation, 100% crescents on biopsy, no pulmonary hemorrhage**: Treat with dialysis alone. Kidney recovery extremely unlikely. DO NOT do plasmapheresis + immunosuppression (futile for kidney, only adds toxicity). BUT if pulmonary hemorrhage develops, MUST start immunosuppression regardless.

6. **Patient with RPGN, ANCA negative, pauci-immune crescentic GN on biopsy**: ANCA-negative pauci-immune GN (27-38% of type 3). Treat same as ANCA-positive disease: steroids + CYC/rituximab.

7. **Alport patient post kidney transplant develops proteinuria + hematuria at 6 months**: De novo anti-GBM disease (3-4% risk). Check anti-GBM antibodies. Biopsy transplant kidney: linear IgG along GBM. Prognosis poor (75% graft failure).

8. **GPA patient in remission on azathioprine, rising PR3-ANCA titers**: Rising ANCA may predict relapse but is NOT sufficient alone to escalate treatment. Increase monitoring frequency. Treat if clinical relapse confirmed. Consider switching maintenance to rituximab.

9. **Woman with hematuria, family history of kidney failure and hearing loss, anterior lenticonus ABSENT**: Could be XLAS carrier. > 90% have microhematuria. Genetic testing for COL4A5 confirms. 12% reach ESRD by age 40. Start ACEi if proteinuria present.

10. **RPGN + low C3 + low C4 + "full house" IF on biopsy**: Lupus nephritis (class III/IV +/- V). Check ANA, anti-dsDNA, anti-Sm. Treatment per lupus nephritis guidelines: pulse steroids + CYC or MMF induction.
