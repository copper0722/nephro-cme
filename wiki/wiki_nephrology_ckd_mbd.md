# CKD-Mineral and Bone Disorder (CKD-MBD)

## Exam Logic Summary

CKD-MBD is extremely high-yield. The exam tests: (1) pathophysiology cascade (↓ 1,25-D → ↓ Ca²⁺ → ↑ PTH → ↑ FGF-23 → ↑ phosphate), (2) PTH targets by CKD stage, (3) phosphate binder comparison (Ca-based vs non-Ca), (4) renal osteodystrophy classification (high vs low turnover), (5) calciphylaxis recognition and management, (6) when to use calcimimetics vs vitamin D analogs. Common distractor: treating all ↑ PTH with calcitriol (contraindicated if hyperphosphatemia or hypercalcemia present).

**Textbook ref:** Brenner Ch. 56 (CKD-MBD), Daugirdas Ch. 37-38, KDIGO 2017 CKD-MBD Update (Kidney Int Suppl 2017;7(1):1)

---

## Definition

CKD-MBD = systemic disorder of mineral and bone metabolism due to CKD, characterized by:
1. Abnormalities of Ca²⁺, phosphate, PTH, FGF-23, or vitamin D metabolism
2. Abnormalities of bone turnover, mineralization, volume, linear growth, or strength
3. Extra-osseous calcification (arterial, valvular, myocardial)

Renal osteodystrophy = alteration in bone morphology specifically (the bone component of CKD-MBD).

## Pathophysiology Cascade (MUST KNOW)

```
↓ Nephron mass
  → ↓ Phosphate excretion → hyperphosphatemia
  → ↓ 1-alpha hydroxylase → ↓ 1,25(OH)2D (calcitriol)
    → ↓ Intestinal Ca²⁺ absorption → hypocalcemia
    → ↑ PTH (secondary hyperparathyroidism)
  → ↑ FGF-23 (from osteocytes, earliest marker)
    → ↓ Phosphate reabsorption (compensatory initially)
    → ↓ 1,25(OH)2D production (worsens the cycle)
```

**Key timing:**
- FGF-23 rises FIRST (even before PTH), detectable as early as CKD stage 2
- PTH begins to rise when GFR < 60 mL/min/1.73 m²
- Ca²⁺ and phosphate abnormalities typically appear when GFR < 40
- Secondary hyperparathyroidism present in >= 50% by dialysis initiation

**Exam pearl:** FGF-23 is the earliest biomarker of CKD-MBD. It rises before phosphate, PTH, or calcium change. FGF-23 requires klotho as coreceptor. In CKD, klotho expression ↓, creating FGF-23 resistance.

| Stage 4 | q3-6 months | q3-6 months | q6-12 months | q12 months (more if ↑ PTH) |
| Stage 5/5D | q1-3 months | q1-3 months | q3-6 months | q12 months (more if ↑ PTH) |
| Post-transplant | Weekly until stable | Weekly until stable | Per baseline + progression | -- |

- Also measure 25(OH)D (calcidiol) in CKD 3-5 including 5D [KDIGO Level 2, Grade C]
- Base testing frequency on presence/magnitude of abnormalities and CKD progression rate

## PTH Management by CKD Stage

### CKD 3-5 (Not on Dialysis)

- **Optimal PTH level is NOT KNOWN** [KDIGO]
- If iPTH progressively rising or persistently above upper normal limit → evaluate and correct:
  - Hyperphosphatemia
  - Hypocalcemia
  - Vitamin D deficiency (25(OH)D < 30 ng/mL)
  - High phosphate intake
- Calcitriol and vitamin D analogs should NOT be routinely used [KDIGO Level 2, Grade C]
- Reserve calcitriol/vitamin D analogs for CKD 4-5 with severe and progressive hyperparathyroidism

### CKD 5D (On Dialysis)

- Target iPTH: approximately **2-9 times upper normal limit** of the assay [KDIGO Level 2, Grade C]
  - For most assays (normal 10-65 pg/mL), this means roughly 130-585 pg/mL
- For significant changes in either direction, initiate or evaluate therapies to avoid progression outside this range
- If PTH-lowering required, consider [KDIGO Level 2, Grade B]:
  - Calcimimetics (cinacalcet) -- especially if hypercalcemia present
  - Calcitriol
  - Vitamin D analogs
  - Combination of calcimimetics + calcitriol/vitamin D analogs
- For severe hyperparathyroidism not responding to medical therapy → parathyroidectomy [KDIGO Level 2, Grade B]

**Exam pearl:** PTH target in dialysis is 2-9x ULN, NOT a fixed number. Exam questions use this range. Low PTH (< 2x ULN) suggests adynamic bone disease. Very high PTH (> 9x ULN) suggests osteitis fibrosa cystica.

## Phosphate Management

### Dietary

- Limit dietary phosphate to 800-1500 mg/day in CKD 3-5 [KDIGO Level 2, Grade D]
- Consider phosphate source: organic (meat/dairy) > inorganic (additives/preservatives) in bioavailability
- Education on phosphorus-containing food additives reduces serum P (JAMA 2009)

### Phosphate Binders -- Comparison Table (HIGH-YIELD)

| Binder | Type | Dose | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Calcium acetate** | Ca-based | 667 mg TID with meals | Cheap, effective, ↓ PTH | Hypercalcemia, vascular calcification risk |
| **Calcium carbonate** | Ca-based | 500-1500 mg TID with meals | Cheap, OTC | Same as above; less P-binding per gram |
| **Sevelamer HCl** | Non-Ca, non-Al | 800-1600 mg TID with meals | No Ca load, ↓ LDL, may ↓ mortality | GI side effects (constipation), expensive, ↓ HCO3⁻ |
| **Sevelamer carbonate** | Non-Ca, non-Al | 800-1600 mg TID with meals | Same as HCl but no metabolic acidosis | Expensive |
| **Lanthanum carbonate** | Non-Ca, non-Al | 500-1000 mg TID chewable | Effective, low pill burden | Lanthanum tissue deposition (clinical significance unclear) |
| **Ferric citrate** | Non-Ca, iron-based | 210 mg elemental Fe TID | Also treats iron deficiency | ↑ Iron stores, dark stools |
| **Aluminum hydroxide** | Aluminum-based | Variable | Very effective | AVOID long-term: aluminum toxicity (osteomalacia, encephalopathy, anemia) |

**KDIGO recommendations:**
- Ca-based binders: restrict dose in adults receiving phosphate-lowering treatment [KDIGO Level 2, Grade B]
- Consider non-Ca-based alternatives if: persistent hypercalcemia, arterial calcification, adynamic bone disease, persistently low PTH
- AVOID long-term aluminum-containing binders [KDIGO Level 1, Grade C]
- Avoid dialysate aluminum contamination [KDIGO Level 1, Grade C]
- Dialysate Ca²⁺ concentration: 1.25-1.5 mmol/L (2.5-3.0 mEq/L) [KDIGO Level 2, Grade C]

### Sevelamer vs Calcium -- Key Evidence

- Cochrane 2025 (134 trials, N=20,913): sevelamer associated with ↓ all-cause mortality (RR 0.54, 95% CI 0.32-0.93) vs Ca-based binders in dialysis patients; reduced hypercalcemia (RR 0.3)
- DCOR trial: no significant mortality difference overall; sevelamer ↓ mortality in patients >= 65 (18.2 vs 23.4 per 100 pt-years, p=0.02)
- For CKD 2-5 NOT on dialysis: sevelamer did NOT reduce mortality vs placebo/standard care (Cochrane 2025)

**Exam pearl:** The current evidence favors non-calcium-based binders (especially sevelamer) in DIALYSIS patients. But in CKD NOT on dialysis, the mortality benefit is not established. Calcium-based binders are effective and may be better tolerated.

## Vitamin D in CKD-MBD

### Native Vitamin D (Ergocalciferol/Cholecalciferol)

- Correct 25(OH)D deficiency/insufficiency based on general population recommendations [KDIGO Level 2, Grade C]
- Check 25(OH)D levels in CKD 3-5 and post-transplant

### Active Vitamin D (Calcitriol, Alfacalcidol, Paricalcitol, Doxercalciferol)

- CKD 3-5 (not on dialysis): do NOT routinely use [KDIGO Level 2, Grade C]
- CKD 5D: consider for PTH-lowering if needed, alongside calcimimetics
- Risks: hypercalcemia, hyperphosphatemia, oversuppression of PTH → adynamic bone

---

## Calcimimetics (Cinacalcet, Etelcalcetide)

- Mechanism: allosteric modulator of calcium-sensing receptor (CaSR) on parathyroid gland → ↓ PTH secretion
- Cinacalcet (oral): starting dose 30 mg daily, titrate q2-4 weeks
- Etelcalcetide (IV): given at end of dialysis session, 5 mg TIW
- Indications: secondary hyperparathyroidism in CKD 5D, especially with hypercalcemia (where vitamin D analogs are contraindicated)
- Side effects: hypocalcemia (monitor closely), nausea, QT prolongation
- EVOLVE trial (cinacalcet vs placebo in HD): did not significantly reduce composite of death/CV events in ITT analysis, but adjusted analysis suggested benefit

**Exam pearl:** Use calcimimetics FIRST if PTH is high AND calcium is high. Use vitamin D analogs if PTH is high AND calcium is low/normal. Use combination for refractory cases.

## Renal Osteodystrophy -- Classification (MUST MEMORIZE)

| Type | Bone Turnover | Mineralization | Key Feature | PTH Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Osteitis fibrosa** (cystica) | ↑↑ High | Normal | Classic 2° HPTH, woven bone, marrow fibrosis | Very high |
| **Adynamic bone disease** | ↓↓ Low | Normal (but acellular) | Oversuppression of PTH, ↑ fracture risk | Low |
| **Osteomalacia** | ↓ Low | ↓ Abnormal | Unmineralized osteoid, aluminum toxicity (historical) | Low-normal |
| **Mixed** | ↑ High | ↓ Abnormal | Features of both osteitis fibrosa + osteomalacia | Variable |
| **Mild** | Slightly ↑ | Normal | Slight increase, early CKD-MBD | Mildly elevated |

**TMV classification** (newer): describes Turnover, Mineralization, Volume independently -- does not place into discrete categories.

**Bone biopsy** indicated in CKD 3-5D when:
- Biochemical abnormalities + low BMD and/or fragility fractures [KDIGO Level 2, Grade D]
- Knowledge of type will influence treatment decisions
- Unexplained fractures, persistent bone pain, unexplained hypercalcemia/hypophosphatemia
- Possible aluminum toxicity
- Prior bisphosphonate therapy in CKD-MBD

**Exam pearl:** Adynamic bone disease is the MOST COMMON form of ROD in dialysis patients today (due to aggressive PTH suppression). It presents with low PTH, ↑ fracture risk, and ↑ vascular calcification (bone can't buffer Ca²⁺). Treatment = let PTH rise (reduce Ca-based binders, reduce vitamin D analogs).

## Calciphylaxis (Calcific Uremic Arteriolopathy)

- Life-threatening condition with small vessel calcification, thrombosis, and skin necrosis
- Mortality 60-80% at 1 year
- Risk factors: female sex, obesity, diabetes, warfarin use, Ca-based binders, vitamin D analogs, high Ca x P product
- Presentation: excruciatingly painful, violaceous livedo reticularis → necrotic eschars, typically on abdomen/thighs
- Diagnosis: clinical + skin biopsy (medial calcification of dermal arterioles with intimal fibrosis and thrombosis)
- Management:
  - Stop warfarin → switch to DOAC
  - Stop Ca-based binders → switch to non-Ca binder
  - Sodium thiosulfate IV (25 g in 100 mL NS over 30-60 min after each HD session)
  - Wound care, pain management
  - Cinacalcet for PTH control
  - Intensify dialysis
  - Avoid tissue debridement of viable tissue

**Exam pearl:** Calciphylaxis is a CLINICAL diagnosis. Biopsy confirms but is not always needed if typical presentation. Warfarin is a major risk factor because it inhibits vitamin K-dependent matrix Gla protein (calcification inhibitor). This is the mechanism behind warfarin-associated calciphylaxis.

## Bone Disease After Transplant

- CKD stages 1-5 post-transplant: correct vitamin D deficiency [KDIGO Level 2, Grade C]
- First 12 months post-transplant with eGFR > 30 and low BMD: consider vitamin D, calcitriol/alfacalcidol, and/or antiresorptive agents [KDIGO Level 2, Grade D]
- Bisphosphonates: consider only if hyperparathyroidism corrected, kidney function stable, fracture risk outweighs rare risk of osteonecrosis of jaw or adynamic bone
- Monitor serum Ca²⁺ and phosphorus at least weekly until stable post-transplant [KDIGO Level 1, Grade B]

## High-Yield Exam Scenarios

1. **CKD 4 patient with PTH 250 pg/mL, Ca 8.2, P 6.5**: First correct hyperphosphatemia (phosphate binder with meals) and give native vitamin D. Do NOT start calcitriol yet (P must be controlled first).

2. **Dialysis patient with PTH 1200, Ca 11.5, P 7.2**: Start calcimimetic (cinacalcet). Calcitriol is CONTRAINDICATED (hypercalcemia). Switch to non-Ca phosphate binder. Consider parathyroidectomy if refractory.

3. **Dialysis patient with PTH 50, frequent fractures**: This is adynamic bone disease. Reduce Ca-based binders, stop calcitriol, let PTH rise. PTH < 2x ULN in dialysis = too low.

4. **Painful purple skin lesions on abdomen in obese dialysis patient on warfarin**: Calciphylaxis. Stop warfarin, start sodium thiosulfate, switch to non-Ca binder, cinacalcet for PTH.

5. **CKD 3 patient: which labs to monitor?** Ca, P, PTH at baseline, 25(OH)D. Repeat Ca and P q6-12 months. PTH based on baseline level and CKD progression. ALP annually.

6. **Phosphate binder choice in dialysis patient with CAC score 800**: Non-calcium-based binder (sevelamer or lanthanum). Arterial calcification is a contraindication for Ca-based binders.
