# Wiki: Diagnosis and Management of Dementia

## 1. Definition & Classification
*   **Dementia**: Acquired loss of cognition in $\ge 2$ cognitive domains (traditionally) sufficiently severe to impair **social, occupational, or self-care function**.
    *   *Note*: DSM-5 allows for single-domain impairment if severe (e.g., global aphasia).
*   **Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI)**: Objective cognitive decline on testing, but **functional independence maintained**.
    *   **Amnestic MCI**: Primary deficit in memory.
    *   **Non-amnestic MCI**: Deficit in $\ge 1$ non-memory domain (language, executive function, attention).
    *   **Single vs. Multidomain**: Refers to the number of cognitive areas affected.

## 2. Etiology & Pathophysiology
*   **Mixed Dementia**: Most common presentation. Typically **Alzheimer Disease (AD)** + **Cerebrovascular Disease (VCID)**.
*   **Neurodegenerative Hierarchy (Frequency)**:
    1.  Alzheimer Disease (AD)
    2.  Lewy Body Disease (LBD)
    3.  Frontotemporal Dementia (FTD) $\rightarrow$ *Exam Tip: Consider in young-onset (<65y).*
*   **Risk Factors**:
    *   **Non-modifiable**: $\uparrow$ Age, Female sex, Black race, Hispanic ethnicity, **APOE gene**.
    *   **Modifiable**: HTN, Diabetes, sedentary lifestyle, low cognitive engagement.

## 3. Clinical Manifestations (Exam Logic)
| Stage | Cognitive/Psychological | Behavioral/Physical |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early** | Episodic memory loss (forgetting appointments), Anomia (word-finding), Apathy | Social withdrawal, Disinhibition, Gait instability/Falls |
| **Late** | Working memory loss (cannot use microwave), Global aphasia, Delusions, **Anosognosia** (lack of insight) | Aggression, Visual hallucinations, Wandering, Parkinsonism, Altered sleep-wake cycle |

## 4. Diagnostic Workup
*   **Step 1: History & Screening**:
    *   Patient history + **corroboration from informant** (family/friend).
    *   Mental status exam: Memory, language, attention, visuospatial, executive function, mood.
    *   Brief screening questionnaires for initial triage.
*   **Step 2: Objective Testing**:
    *   **Neuropsychological testing**: Indicated if screening is inconclusive or to differentiate MCI from Dementia.
*   **Step 3: Etiology Identification**:
    *   **Physical Exam**: Focal neuro deficits $\rightarrow$ suggest stroke/vascular.
    *   **Neuroimaging**: Identify atrophy, infarcts, or tumors.
    *   **Advanced Testing (Atypical cases)**: CSF assays or genetic testing if:
        *   Onset < 65 years.
        *   Rapid progression.
        *   Non-episodic memory deficits.

## 5. Management
*   **Non-Pharmacologic (First-line/Adjunct)**:
    *   Cognitive engagement (reading), physical exercise (walking), socialization.
*   **Pharmacologic (Symptomatic relief only)**:
    *   **Acetylcholinesterase Inhibitors (e.g., Donepezil)**: Mild to severe AD.
    *   **Memantine**: Moderate to severe AD (monotherapy or add-on).
    *   **Rivastigmine**: Specifically indicated for **Parkinson disease dementia**.
